Saskatchewan 21st Century Initiative

Topics

Prefer to browse by theme? Each accordion below summarizes how the Initiative tackles a core challenge, with quick hits on delivery plans, measurements, and next milestones.

Housing

Ending the housing crisis means stabilizing rents now and scaling new, climate-ready homes across the province.

  • Five-year rent freeze, renter registry, and emergency repair grants keep people in place while stock is rebuilt.
  • Saskatchewan Housing Corporation acquires, retrofits, and pre-leases buildings using 21-day permit clocks.
  • SaskHomes pattern book + SMC prefab factory deliver Century Code–compliant homes at scale.
Universal Health

Head-to-toe care becomes a true public utility—covering pharmacare, dental, vision, mental health, and home care with $0 at point of service.

  • Universal Health Levy funds recruitment bonuses, seat expansions, and community health centres across Saskatchewan.
  • Benefit councils with clinicians and patients manage formularies, fee guides, and transparent wait-time dashboards.
  • Virtual and in-person care standards guarantee attachment within 90 days and publish access metrics in real time.
Saskatchewan Dignity Dividend

The Dignity Dividend is a monthly $15,000/year benefit that guarantees a floor of economic dignity for residents.

  • Funded through the Saskatchewan Dignity Fund with a 36-month liquidity buffer and fossil-free investments.
  • Phase-in pairs the dividend with METR safeguards so people always keep more than they contribute.
  • Quarterly dashboards publish enrolment, fiscal balance, and impact metrics across communities.
Saskatchewan Century Corps (SCC)

The SCC is a dignity-wage skills corps that trains and deploys residents to build homes, care, climate, and digital infrastructure.

  • Priority placement for fossil-fuel workers, with relocation grants, tuition coverage, and pension bridging.
  • Streams cover Care & Community, Digital, Infrastructure, and Resilience builds tied to Initiative targets.
  • Works alongside SaskJobs and SaskFinancial to support worker-led co-ops and fair labour transitions.
Taxes & Revenue

Progressive taxation and new public finance tools fund universal programs without squeezing working families.

  • Modernized personal and corporate brackets lower rates for small business while increasing top-end contributions.
  • Sask Bonds let residents fund climate rail, housing, and energy builds with transparent use-of-proceeds reporting.
  • Same-business group rules close loopholes and ensure profitable conglomerates pay their share.
Dignity

The Saskatchewan Human Dignity Act makes dignity a legal duty across ministries, Crowns, and partners.

  • Expands the Human Rights Code to explicitly protect gender identity, neurodiversity, and social condition.
  • Creates a charter of rights—from housing and food to reconciliation and democracy—that drives every bill.
  • Requires public services to publish dignity compliance plans and dashboards residents can audit.
Reconciliation

Nothing moves without Free, Prior, and Informed Consent—co-governance is written into legislation and funding.

  • Saskatchewan Council for Reconciliation co-chairs implementation, controls capacity funding, and issues binding directions.
  • Consent Protocol registry documents agreements, timelines, and benefit sharing for every project.
  • Land Back and MMIWG secretariats receive statutory funding through the Saskatchewan Reconciliation Fund.
Power & Energy

Clean, public energy with predictable permitting replaces fossil dependency over a staged timeline.

  • SaskPower builds microgrids, storage, and clean generation toward a 2055 zero-emission grid.
  • SaskEnergy rolls out ambient thermal loops and heat-pump ready districts with dig-once corridor partners.
  • Cleanup bonds, FPIC, and subsidy phase-outs hold resource developers accountable from day one.
Resilience

Every community is within 30 minutes of a resilience hub with clean air, power, comms, and supplies.

  • 24/7 multilingual alerting spans SaskAlert, radio, cell broadcast, and satellite redundancy.
  • Hubs sit in schools, libraries, and community centres with solar, batteries, air filtration, and cots.
  • Parametric insurance plus the Saskatchewan Resiliency Fund pay out quickly after floods, fires, or storms.
Digital Sovereignty

Residents own their data, code, and digital services—no more vendor lock-in or offshore hosting.

  • SaskOS, SaskTel email, and passkey security replace proprietary stacks across government.
  • All sensitive data must live in Saskatchewan data centres with transparent incident reporting.
  • Saskatchewan Digital Services builds open-source tools with CodeShare Civics releases for public review.
Financial Sovereignty

Public finance tools backstop social programs, worker co-ops, and climate infrastructure.

  • SaskFinancial Group offers no-fee banking, retrofit loans, and co-op financing with provincial guarantees.
  • Dignity Fund Authority manages long-horizon investments and quarterly public dashboards.
  • Crown dividend reforms split predictable revenue between the General Revenue Fund and strategic funds.
Resources

Critical minerals, helium, and aggregates are developed with public ownership, FPIC, and thin margins returned to residents.

  • Saskatchewan Natural Resource Corporation competes in extraction while sharing governance with Indigenous partners.
  • Cleanup bonding, reclamation timelines, and transparent royalty tiers keep projects accountable.
  • Revenues feed the Reconciliation Fund, Dignity Fund, and climate transition builds.
Democracy (MMP)

Mixed-member proportional representation and open civic repositories put democratic power back in people’s hands.

  • One ballot, two votes: local MLA plus ranked regional list for fair seat distribution.
  • Ban on private lobbying, strict donation caps, and mandatory publication of meeting materials.
  • CodeShare Civics hosts laws, regs, datasets, and petitions with signed commits and response deadlines.
Food & Agriculture

Food security, fair farm income, and regenerative practices are delivered together.

  • Floor prices and deficiency payments stabilize farm revenue while rewarding regenerative agriculture.
  • SaskGrocery formats—supermarkets, markets, minis, and mobile—eliminate food deserts province-wide.
  • Right-to-repair, rapid payments, and wholesale transparency keep independent producers competitive.
