The

New Century Party

The NCP is a new political movement for Saskatchewan, built on a simple, powerful idea: we must meet this moment, together.

For generations, Saskatchewan has lived by a simple motto: We do it ourselves. We didn't wait to be profitable enough. We built public options, from healthcare to education, power to heating, communications to insurance, to ensure everyone has access to the services they deserve.

Our platform for a 21st Century Saskatchewan continues this tradition. It is a detailed blueprint made of 13 bills, developed in the open for all to see. We don't have all the answers, and there is no doing this alone. We invite you to read the plans, challenge our ideas, and help us build a future of hope and prosperity for all.

A 21st Century Saskatchewan

The Saskatchewan 21st Century Initiative is a roadmap. Each bill answers a specific pressure of the polycrisis and reinforces the others. Scroll through the spine to see how the plan restores dignity, honours treaties, and future-proofs our province.

  1. 01 · The Saskatchewan Human Dignity Act

    Codifies human dignity as the government’s first duty, expands human rights protections, and embeds a legal duty of care across every ministry.

    Dignity & Rights

  2. 02 · The Saskatchewan Reconciliation and Treaty Renewal Act

    Implements UNDRIP and mandatory Free, Prior and Informed Consent, invests in Indigenous-led infrastructure, and funds a permanent Reconciliation Council.

    Treaty Partnership

  3. 03 · The Saskatchewan Universal Health Act

    Delivers head-to-toe care—mental health, dental, vision, prescriptions—through a single payer system financed by a universal health levy.

    Health & Care

  4. 04 · The Saskatchewan Housing Crisis Act

    Freezes rents, rebuilds and acquires public housing through the Saskatchewan Housing Corporation, and accelerates permits for Century Code homes.

    Homes for All

  5. 05 · The Saskatchewan 21st Century Connected Communities Act

    Creates walkable neighbourhoods, reboots STC for transit and freight, and coordinates ambient energy loops, fibre, and bike-first streets province-wide.

    Connected Communities

  6. 06 · The Saskatchewan Fair Labour and Just Transition Act

    Sets a 30-hour standard week, a $25 wage floor, and launches the Saskatchewan Century Corps to retrain workers while delivering public projects.

    Work & Transition

  7. 07 · The Saskatchewan Lifelong Learning Act

    Guarantees class size supports, free K-12 and post-secondary access, expands the Distance Learning Centre, and modernizes libraries as public tech hubs.

    Learning

  8. 08 · The Saskatchewan Financial Sovereignty Act

    Funds a $20,000 Dignity Dividend, builds the Saskatchewan Sovereignty Fund, and creates Sask Financial Group for public banking and green bonds.

    Economic Security

  9. 09 · The Saskatchewan Food Sovereignty Act

    Stands up the Saskatchewan Agriculture Board, guarantees fair crop prices, expands SaskGrocery, and enforces right-to-repair for farm equipment.

    Food & Farms

  10. 10 · The Saskatchewan Digital Sovereignty Act

    Moves government data onshore, develops SaskOS and open digital services, and completes fibre to every home through SaskTel.

    Digital Commons

  11. 11 · The Saskatchewan Resource and Energy Sovereignty Act

    Ends fossil fuel giveaways, scales clean power with SaskPower, mandates FPIC for resource projects, and builds microgrids and cleanup bonds.

    Energy Transition

  12. 12 · The Saskatchewan Democratic Renewal Act

    Introduces mixed-member proportional elections, bans pay-to-play lobbying, enforces full donation transparency, and strengthens Elections Saskatchewan.

    Democracy

  13. 13 · The Saskatchewan Disaster Resiliency Act

    Builds province-wide resilience hubs, mandates modern emergency plans, funds the Saskatchewan Resiliency Fund, and equips SCC teams for response.

    Climate Resilience

Institutions that make it real

The bills unlock a network of public institutions that keep wealth, data, and accountability in Saskatchewan hands.

Saskatchewan Century Corps

Permanent public workforce delivering retrofits, care, and climate projects with a 30-hour week, on-the-job training, and a dignified wage ladder.

SaskGrocery & SAB

Province-wide public grocery network and agriculture board that stabilizes prices, backs regenerative farming, and ends food deserts.

Sask Digital Services

Builds SaskOS, public-facing apps, and local data centres so residents own their digital lives and ministries deliver modern services.

Saskatchewan Sovereignty Fund

Invests resource wealth into the Dignity Dividend, reconciliation, and transition projects, shielding public services from boom-bust cycles.

Saskatchewan Council for Reconciliation

Shared governance with Indigenous Nations to enforce FPIC, steward the Reconciliation Fund, and publish transparent progress dashboards.

Saskatchewan Emergency Response Agency

Coordinates resilience hubs, early warning, and parametric insurance so every community can withstand climate shocks.
