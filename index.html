The

New Century Party

The NCP is a new political movement for Saskatchewan, built on a simple, powerful idea: we must meet this moment, together.

For generations, Saskatchewan has lived by a simple motto: We do it ourselves. We didn't wait to be profitable enough. We built public options, from healthcare to education, power to heating, communications to insurance, to ensure everyone has access to the services they deserve.

Our platform of 21 Policies for the 21st Century Saskatchewan continues this tradition. It is a detailed blueprint, developed in the open for all to see. We don't have all the answers, and there is no doing this alone. We invite you to read the plans, challenge our ideas, and help us build a future of hope and prosperity for all.

The Saskatchewan Dignity Dividend (SDD)

We will replace the inadequate Saskatchewan Income Support (SIS) with a new dividend meant for not just the least of us, but the vast majority of us.

The SDD is a simple monthly benefit for any resident earning under $100,000 annually.

It starts at $1667/month for those earning $0 annually and is reduced only by 20 cents for every dollar earned. That means that work always pays better, but it no longer controls your dignity.

Universal Health

Saskatchewan made a promise over half a century ago.

A Single Payer Plan that provides high quality medical services at $0 point of care.

Its time we deliver on that promise in full.

The New Coverage:

  • Mental Health.
  • Vision Care
  • Hearing Care
  • Pharmacare
  • Dental Care
  • Community Care
  • Long-term Care

Paid for by a new payroll tax replacing the private insurance you and your boss pay now.

Just bring your Health Card. That's it.

Reconciliation

An NCP government shall require the full implementation of all 94 Calls to Action from the Truth and Reconciliation Commission as well as the United Nations Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples.

Meaning all projects affecting Indigenous Nations must acquire the Free, Prior, and Informed Consent of those Nations.

We know that reconciliation is not something dictated by the government, but a journey. A path shared in partnership and co-development with the Indigenous peoples who know this place we call home best.

Housing Crisis

Housing is a human right, not a speculative asset. The housing crisis must come to an end. The NCP is committed to ending it.

Short-Term:

  • A Five Year Rent Freeze.

  • Rebuild public stock through SHC pre-leases, rapid rebuild, and housing-first placements.

  • Designate Priority Housing Zones

  • A new Rental Bill of Rights
    • Rent Control
    • Abolish No Fault Evictions
    • Winter Eviction Ban
    • Rental Registry

Long-Term:

  • Standard Pre-Approved Housing Designs (SaskHomes)

  • Modular, Pre-Fab Housing Factory in province

  • SHC shall order 100,000 Units for public housing.
