The Problem

As affordability worsens, people become more and more desperate.

The Promise

The NCP will treat addictions not as moral failing, but as the health crisis that it is.

The NCP will treat the unhoused not as a nuisance but as government failure.

And the NCP will ensure the right people are sent for the right circumstances.

The Plan

Within 24 months of forming government, the NCP will put forward amendments to:

  • The Safer Communities and Neighbourhoods Act.
  • The Police Act, 1990.
  • And related regulations governing first responders and dispatch standards.

Making Saskatchewan's public safety system in line with a health-first, dignity-first model.

These amendments will address three key areas to make our communities safer:

Houselessness

  • The NCP commits to a housing first solution, managed by the Ministry of Social Services, under a unified Housing First caseload, working directly with the Saskatchewan Housing Corporation to ensure priority placements for the unhoused or those living in encampments, and those escaping domestic violence, into newly renovated or acquired public housing as it becomes available.
  • Encampments will not be cleared unless there is secured private rooms, storage, and transport available for each encampment resident.
  • See more in policy 05 - Housing Crisis

Addictions

  • Involuntary addictions treatment will be abolished in Saskatchewan. Treatment must be based on consent, evidence, and dignity.
  • The NCP will work with the federal government and Health Canada to decriminalize small personal possession of illicit drugs, seeking a Saskatchewan-wide exemption so that federal law matches a health-first approach. Addition is not a crime.
  • Provincially regulated police services will be instructed, where there is no public safety risk, to treat simple possession of small amounts of illicit substances for personal use as their lowest priority issue. Instead, officers will be expected to
    • Issue warnings
    • Provide referrals and health-based responses.
    • Focus on enforcement on trafficking, exploitation, and serious violence.
  • First responders will be instructed to focus on overdose prevention and connection to care, and will not automatically seize small amounts of substances for personal use unless there is an immediate safety concern, such as risk to children.
  • All provincially regulated or certified first aid equipment kits must contain overdose reversal medications like naloxone.
  • The Saskatchewan Health Authority shall open and run 24/7 addictions and sobering centres in every major region. These centres will:
    • Provide same day Opioid Agonist Therapy (OAT).
    • Offer same day and outpatient detox treatments.
    • Make warm handoffs to The Ministry of Social Services for housing and other supports.
    • Operate a 24/7 addictions counselling hotline.
    • Provide free, no questions, asked overdose reversal medications like naloxone to anyone.
  • These services will be fully covered under policy 03 - Universal Health as medically necessary mental health and addictions care.

Policing and First Responders

  • The Saskatchewan Marshals will be disbanded. Their funding will be redirected to a new team of first responders called Community Crisis Officers.
  • Community Crisis Officers, managed by the Ministry of Health and the Saskatchewan Health Authority, will be made up of professionals such as:
    • Mental health workers.
    • Social workers.
    • Paramedics.
    • Peer support workers with lived experience.
  • New provincial dispatch standards and agreements that will ensure that 911 dispatchers can triage calls by risk and need:
    • Police will only be dispatched to higher-risk calls, or when specifically requested to accompany Community Crisis Officers, or other first responders.
    • Non-violent mental health, intoxication, welfare checks, and similar calls will go first to Community Crisis Officers or community paramedicine, not to police.
  • All police officers in Saskatchewan will be bound by clear de-escalation requirements and a duty to intervene when a colleague is escalating a situation unnecessarily.
  • Militarized equipment will be restricted to properly trained Emergency Response Teams (ERT) under strict rules, and never used for routine policing.
  • All police officers in Saskatchewan must wear body cameras, with clear policies for:
    • Privacy and data protection.
    • When footage must be reviewed.
    • How footage is used in compliant and discipline.
  • Random street checks or carding will be prohibited.
  • Policy services will be required to collect stop-level data and publish it every quarter. This data must include:
    • Age.
    • Race / Indigenous Identity (self reported).
    • Outcome.
    • Context of the stop.
    • And additional factors as determined by regulation.
  • All police officers in Saskatchewan must receive ongoing training in:
    • Anti-Profiling.
    • Anti-Racism.
    • Trauma-informed practice.
    • Preventing abuse of power.

The Funding

The new mobile crisis first responders, Community Crisis Officers, will be funded through:

  • The Universal Health Levy as part of the budget for mental health and community care.
  • The existing budget allocated to the now-disbanded Saskatchewan Marshals.

Everyday policing and public safety funding will continue to come from the General Revenue Fund, with a shift in priorities to health-first and community-based responses.
