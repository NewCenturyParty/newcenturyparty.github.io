The Problem

Saskatchewan does not capture and save enough of its natural resource wealth.

Saskatchewan's vibrant Credit Union Ecosystem is in need of modernization.

The Promise

The NCP will ensure Saskatchewan resource wealth is captured and secured for generations to come.

The NCP will create a wholesale development trust to ensure Saskatchewan's money is stored and used within Saskatchewan's borders.

The Plan

Within 12 months of forming government, the NCP will introduce the following two bills:

  • The Saskatchewan Sovereignty Fund Act
  • The Saskatchewan Financial Group Act
  • Amendments to The Credit Union Act

With these Acts, Saskatchewan will be able to commit to a financially sovereign future.

The Saskatchewan Sovereignty Fund Act:

  • A new Crown corporation to be known as the Saskatchewan Sovereignty Fund Agency (SSFA) will be mandated to create and manage a new, permanent, intergenerational endowment fund to be known as the Saskatchewan Sovereignty Fund (SSF).
  • The SSF will be funded through resource royalty revenues.
    • 15% of resource royalty revenue collected from the base rate will be sent to the SSF.
    • 50% of the resource royalty revenues collected from the windfall rate will be sent to the SSF
    • For more on resource royalties see Policy 21 - Funding: Taxes, Royalties, Bonds, and METR.
  • The principal capital cannot be withdrawn or spent without a super-majority vote in favour in the Legislature.
  • A percentage of the accrued annual interest of the SSF will be spent as use of proceeds funding to the General Revenue Fund.
    • These interest payments will be split four ways and by law can only be spent on certain programs:
      • 59% must be spent on the Saskatchewan Dignity Dividend (SDD).
        • For more about the SDD see Policy 02 - The Saskatchewan Dignity Dividend.
      • 15% must be spent on reconciliation funding.
        • For more on Reconciliation measures see Policy 04 - Reconciliation.
      • 15% on resilience and just transition funding.
        • For more on resilience see Policy 20 - Disaster Resilience.
        • For more on Just Transition see Policy 10 - Just Transition.
      • 10% on Debt Servicing.
        • For more on funding and SaskBonds see Policy 21 - Funding: Taxes, Royalties, Bonds, and METR.
      • 1% must be spent on administration of the fund and disbursement of payments.
  • Investments must be low risk and stable.
    • No investments in fossil fuels, tobacco, weapons, or other morally questionable industries.
    • Investments must be diversified across multiple sectors and jurisdictions to ensure stability and growth.
    • All investments must be reported transparently to the public quarterly.

The Saskatchewan Financial Group Act:

  • A Crown corporation will be created and known as the Saskatchewan Financial Group (SFG).
  • The SFG will be prohibited from funding or investing in any and all fossil fuel, tobacco, weapons, or other morally questionable industries.
  • The SFG will be mandated to act as a central depository for the Government of Saskatchewan and its Crowns. Meaning Saskatchewan money will stay within Saskatchewan to fund Saskatchewan.
  • Credit Unions will be offered the opportunity to join a federated financial system backed by the SFG.
    • Credit Unions would have access to an SFG developed, world-class, secure, digital banking platform, built in partnership with the Saskatchewan Digital Services (SDS).
      • This would allow Credit Unions to easily, using standard tools, produce their own apps and websites to give their members the modern online banking services they've come to expect without exorbitant vendor fees.
      • For more about the SDS see Policy 16 - Digital Sovereignty.
    • Credit Unions would have access to low cost capital backed by the Government of Saskatchewan's deposit base, so long as they commit to passing on the savings to their customers.
  • The SFG will be mandated to maintain liquidity sufficient for daily Credit Union operations, as well as purchase investment instruments, including Provincial Treasury Bills or Bonds to manage liquidity and support provincial financing.

The Funding

The funding for operating the SSF will come from using 1% of the interest payments for administration and operating costs.

The funding for operating the SFG will come from General Revenues, offset by investment returns.
