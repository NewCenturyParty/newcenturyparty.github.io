The Problem

America owns the entirety of your personal government data.

The Promise

The NCP will use the tools we already have available and build out a world class flag in the digital ground for Saskatchewan and Indigenous Communities.

The Plan

Within 12 months of forming government, the NCP will introduce:

  • The Saskatchewan Digital Services Act.
  • The Saskatchewan Data Sovereignty Act.
  • Amendments to The Saskatchewan Telecommunications Act.

This will allow Saskatchewan to enter the digital age and have a sovereign place within it.

The Saskatchewan Digital Services Act:

  • A Crown corporation will be made and known as the Saskatchewan Digital Services (SDS).
  • The Board of the SDS must include Saskatchewan Indigenous Governing Body representation.
  • The SDS will be mandated to build out the sovereign digital state of Saskatchewan, including:
    • Modernizing all Government of Saskatchewan Services so as to be easily doable online.
    • A Saskatchewan Tech Stack (SaskStack) that allows the SDS to quickly and easily build custom apps for every ministry, agency, and crown of the government, and allows for easy hand off to SaskBuilds and Procurement for daily operations, while avoiding vendor lock-in.
      • This means a single login system, a unified data architecture, single UX/UI, single encryption module, and more, all running locally, offline, on government computers, syncing to local SaskTel servers only as needed.
    • A configured, secure fork of a reliable linux distribution Operating System (OS) to build out SaskOS.
      • After a phased transition, but within 10 years, all Government of Saskatchewan computers must be running SaskOS exclusively.
      • The new apps for each ministry must be compatible on SaskOS for instant offline use.
      • SaskOS will be made to look familiar to a traditional OS and be as intuitive and accessible as possible.
    • Maintaining an open source policy.
      • Open source offline alternatives will be used for necessary office work like word processing, spreadsheet management, presentations, or web browsing.
    • Creating additional apps and services for use outside of government.
      • The SaskBonds purchasing portal.
      • The Saskatchewan Library Authority online portal for borrowing and browsing.
      • The Saskatchewan Transport Company's app and website for ticketing and notifications.
      • The CodeShare Civics online repository.
      • And more.
    • Privacy, Security, and Transparency
      • All code, excluding sensitive data, must be hosted openly on a Government of Saskatchewan CodeShare Civics Repository for all to see and openly audit.
        • For more about CodeShare Civics see Policy 18 - Democratic Renewal.
      • White Hat Hackers will be contracted to help secure everything.
      • Data must be stored and secured using Zero-Trust, ultra secure, privacy principles, in local servers as soon as possible.
      • All employees will be given training, in negotiation with the union(s), to ensure they know how to properly operate the new technology.
      • All employees must use new password and multi-factor authentication protocols with physical pass-key support.

The Saskatchewan Data Sovereignty Act:

  • Within 10 years all Government of Saskatchewan data, including its personal information on residents, must be stored and located within local Saskatchewan data servers. This includes any needed cloud infrastructure for Government of Saskatchewan operations.
  • Ownership, Control, Access, and Possession (OCAP) principles apply to all data of First Nation residents of Saskatchewan, meaning their data must belong to them alone, with the Government of Saskatchewan only being a Sovereign Host to the data. The First Nations will always own the lock and keys.

Amendments to The Saskatchewan Telecommunications Act:

  • SaskTel will be mandated to remain net-neutral.
  • SaskTel will be given a new mandate to procure and operate local sovereign data server centres in the province.
    • SaskTel will create and help operate geographically redundant Sovereign Data Centres, including dedicated infrastructure for:
      • Primary and backup server centres exclusively to be used for Indigenous Data, with total access and control handed off to Indigenous governing bodies.
      • Primary and backup server centres for all Government of Saskatchewan personal and private data.
      • Primary and backup server centres for its encrypted, enterprise grade, email services.
    • These data servers will be ultra secure, encrypted, and private. No one but SaskTel and the relevant Indigenous governing body will have access to this data, and it will only be shared otherwise upon court order.
  • SaskTel will also be given the mandate to remake its email service to be a modern, above enterprise grade, email provider for the Government of Saskatchewan.
    • The reimagined SaskTel email will be an enterprise grade alternative to big tech's offering.
      • This will allow the Government of Saskatchewan to securely and privately, via end to end encryption, send emails internally, as well externally by single point encryption.
    • The reimagined SaskTel email service will include standard modern email services such as todo lists, calendars, and invites, and more.
    • The reimagined SaskTel email service will also be open to the public so that Saskatchewan residents may also enjoy the ultra secure and private email service.

The Funding

The upfront funding for capital intensive projects will be funded by SaskBonds, with operating costs coming from the General Revenue Fund.

Cost savings will come via lack of numerous and exorbitant vendor fees.

What It Means For You

It means a modern, digital friendly, government that meets you where you are.

It means your data isn't used to train an AI model making the internet worse.

It means the dignity in your data being your own, private and protected.
