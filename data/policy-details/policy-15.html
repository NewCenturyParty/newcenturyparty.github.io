The Problem

Saskatchewan is not taking advantage of the resources available to it.

The Promise

The NCP will capture the future resource sector ensuring the benefits go to the people.

The NCP will modernize the power grid and power generation to meet the needs of the 21st Century.

The Plan

Within 12 months of forming government, the NCP will introduce the following:

  • The Saskatchewan Natural Resources Corporation Act
  • Amendments to the Power Corporation Act.

This will allow us to benefit from the resources of the future and build out the power grid of the future.

The Saskatchewan Natural Resources Corporation Act:

  • The Saskatchewan Natural Resources Corporation:
    • A new Crown corporation will be made and known as the Saskatchewan Natural Resources Corporation (SNRC).
    • The SNRC board must be composed of no less than 50% representation from Saskatchewan Indigenous governing bodies.
    • The SNRC will have a mandate of helping to set up and operate new major resource projects in partnership with industry and local Indigenous governing bodies, communities and organizations.
    • All new resource projects will be mandated to be exclusively unionized.
    • The SNRC is prohibited from working in any capacity on Oil, Gas, and Coal projects.
    • The Value Add mandate:
      • The SNRC is mandated to build or partner with the Saskatchewan Manufacturing Corporation (SMC) to build a Uranium Refinery and Fuel Fabrication Facility in Saskatchewan.
        • This fuel will be used to power SaskPower's CANDU reactors, with excess capacity exported to other Canadian jurisdictions.
        • For more about the SMC see Policy 19 - Manufacturing.
        • See below for more on SaskPower.
  • Dormant Lease Levy:
    • A steep, escalating Dormant Lease Tax will be applied to mineral leases held for speculative purposes without active development plans.
    • Failure to pay or begin development after three years of the new Dormant Lease Tax results in the lease reverting to the Crown.
  • For all new major resource development projects in Saskatchewan, the province reserves the right to purchase a majority equity working interest stake of no less than 51%, at audited cost to date, with back-in rights.
    • This means the Province will pay at least 51% of the audited exploration and development costs incurred to date, not 51% of potential value.

SaskPower:

  • SaskPower will be mandated to modernize the entire electrical grid to be able to withstand the needs of the 21st century.
    • The new grid must be able to withstand extreme weather, be it prolonged or acute events.
    • The grid must be able to sustain large-scale EV charging.
    • SaskPower will install EV and eBike charging stations in multiple convenient locations throughout communities across Saskatchewan.
    • The province must be both connected and segmented into local and regional grids.
    • Critical infrastructure must have islandable power.
      • See Policy - 20 Disaster Resilience.
    • The new provincial power grid must be layered with other grids, like neighbourhood micro grids, municipal grids, and local or regional grids, to ensure there is no single point of failure.
  • SaskPower must begin the process of building a Virtual Power Plant.
    • A new rate class will be created alongside the technology platform to link household solar, batteries, EVs, and other smart appliances, into a unified grid asset.
    • Anyone with the capable technology can apply to be part of the VPP.
    • Those who sign up to join the VPP will be offered a no money down, sliding scale, on bill repayment plan for the following residential or business upgrades:
      • New, ultra efficient, electrified household or business appliances.
      • New roof top solar panels and household or industrial battery packs.
      • New Electric Vehicle Charger Installations.
      • New Smart Thermostats.
      • New Smart Appliances.
      • and more.
    • All new appliances or solar panels and batteries must be installed as to be easily upgradable as the technology develops and matures.
    • Those who sign up to be part of the VPP will have their solar and battery storage, and potentially other smart appliances, connected to the grid, which can then use that power to stabilize power draw throughout the province, especially during emergencies.
    • Any power used from household power will be credited on bill to the electricity provider.
  • SaskPower must immediately begin expansion of its energy generation:
    • Solar Farms will be built and maintained throughout the province, in particular the south west.
    • Wind Farms will be built and maintained throughout the province.
    • New Modern CANDU nuclear reactors will be built and operated throughout the province, providing new, clean, and abundant baseload power.
      • These new reactors will use fuel fabricated in Saskatchewan at the Uranium Refinery and Fuel Fabrication Facility built by the SNRC and SMC.
        • See above for more on the SNRC.

The Funding

The primary upfront funding for these major projects will come from SaskBond revenues and funding from the General Revenue Fund for daily operations.

Ongoing funding for the SNRC will come from its share of profits from resource projects, and ongoing funding for SaskPower will come from its revenues.

Costs will be offset by the Dormant Lease Levy on speculative mineral leases, as well as the lower operating costs of modern power generation and a more efficient grid.
