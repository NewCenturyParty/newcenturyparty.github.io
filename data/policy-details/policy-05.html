The Problem

Housing has become a speculative commodity, influencing both supply and demand, price and availability, in ways that do not benefit anyone outside of shareholders.

The Promise

An NCP government will end the housing crisis.

The Plan

Short-Term

Within three months of forming government, the NCP will introduce The Saskatchewan Rental Rights Act.

The Saskatchewan Rental Rights Act will include:

  • A Residential Rent Freeze
    • Legislating a rent freeze encompassing all residential landlords in the province.
    • Includes a sunset date of five years.
    • New builds are exempt so long as their starting rents are not above the median for the area.
    • Capital-repair-pass-through for life/safety, building envelope, and accessibility upgrades, under clear rules.
  • A Rental Registry
    • All landlords in the province must register in a public registry by submitting information about their property and proof of ownership.
    • The registry will be public, with maps and lists of rentals showing key information so renters can easily see and make an informed choice on where to live.
    • Landlords with over 100 units must also apply to a separate registry with the province and are subject to extra reporting and oversight.
    • If a landlord doesn't register they cannot receive any grants or rebates, or legally evict their tenants.
  • Rent Control
    • After the freeze, the maximum amount a residential landlord may increase their rent is by the annual general CPI of Saskatchewan + 0.5%.
  • A Winter Eviction Ban
    • Starting October 1 to April 30 of the following year, residential landlords are prohibited from evicting tenants, except in cases of serious health and safety concerns or severe, proven lease violations, as determined by an independent tribunal.
  • Abolish No Fault Evictions.
    • Landlords may not evict tenants for reasons unrelated to the tenants conduct.
    • Landlords are prohibited from exaggerating or misrepresenting infractions to the rental agreement just to evict a tenant.
    • In cases of the owner wanting to move themselves or family into the unit, or major renovations, the tenant must be given 6 months written notice, compensation, and right of return protections.
  • Landlord repair rebates
    • Rebates will be available from the Government of Saskatchewan for owners who complete safety, accessibility, or energy upgrades, including any Century Codes compliance upgrades.
    • See Policy 06 - 21st Century Connected Communities for more on Century Codes.
  • Vacancy Tax
    • A vacancy tax will be put in place on long-term empty and unused residential units, as to ensure speculators and profiteers pay for keeping livable homes off the market.
    • Primary residences and short, documented transition periods will be exempt under clear rules.

The Saskatchewan Housing Corporation (SHC):

  • The SHC will be given major new investment and be directed to complete a rapid rebuild of their public housing stock by both renovating existing units and purchasing or long-term master leasing existing housing units.

  • The SHC will hire and use prequalified contractors to immediately begin inspecting and renovating every unit within SHC's housing supply, starting with the most neglected and distressed units first.

  • The SHC will also offer to pre-lease 30% of housing units in new developments for the next 10 years should the starting rents are no higher than the median for the area, as determined by provincial data. The operations and management of these units will be co-managed, with statutory funding from the General Revenue Fund.

Saskatchewan Government Insurance (SGI):

  • SGI will be instructed to offer optional renters and housing insurance as part of its main operations, making sure that such insurance is as cheap and ubiquitous as its auto insurance.

  • The rates will vary to reward upgrades: weatherization, heat pumps, solar power, battery storage, Century Codes compliance, and other improvements will qualify homeowners for lower premiums. We will reward those who invest in the future.

Housing First Solutions:

  • New and newly renovated public housing stock will be offered first to those who are currently homeless, and those escaping domestic violence.

  • The Ministry of Social Services shall be instructed to create a single coordinated case list to find and qualify houseless people, with manageable caseloads for the social workers as to ensure clients do not get thrown around.

  • Housing encampments cannot be disbanded unless there are real placements of people and their property. This means a private room and secure storage with transport to get there. Encampment residents will be given first priority placements into new SHC units as they become available.

Long-Term

Saskatchewan needs homes that will meet the needs of its people where they are, both today and in the future. An NCP government will immediately start creating a standardized housing design book for Saskatchewan designed homes.

These SaskHomes will have universal, modular, designs so that they can be built easily on site by hand, the traditional way we know how, and also off site, in a new prefab modular housing factory.

  • See Policy 19 - Manufacturing for more.

And the Saskatchewan Housing Corporation will develop and acquire 100,000 units for public housing, ensuring the people always have a home in Saskatchewan.

SaskHomes:

  • Universal, modular, housing designs for easy construction both on and off site.
  • Standardized supplies and materials so we can share, repair, and build quickly.
  • Material Passports so we know what we used, where, and when it's time to upgrade.
  • Accessible by design so no matter your ability you can live with dignity and comfort.
  • Must be approved by right wherever provincial standards apply.
  • Must be 100% Century Codes Compliant, including being Passive.
    • See Policy 06 - 21st Century Connected Communities for more on Century Codes.
  • Designed to hook up to SaskEnergy's new thermal loops.
    • See Policy 06 - 21st Century Connected Communities for more on SaskEnergy.
  • Includes a variety of possible housing types:
    • Walk Ups
    • Stacked Townhouses
    • Six plex corner infill
    • Accessible seniors mid-rise
    • "Single" Family Home with two units (Upstairs and Downstairs)

Permitting and Zoning:

  • New Provincial Standard Plans and Universal Designs
    • No Parking Minimums
    • Max Heights / Size per street / area
    • Privacy Standards
    • Bike Storage
    • Lot coverage rules
    • Frontage / Designs guidelines
  • Grants will be given to municipalities that agree to phase out exclusive Single Family Zoning and adopt Provincial Standard Plans.

Priority Housing Zones:

  • Located around downtowns, main streets, transit and commercial corridors, school districts, and more.
  • Must encompass over 35% of all available residential zoning.
  • Established in communities across the province in areas where housing is needed most.
  • Housing that meets the Provincial Standard Plans are approved by right
  • SaskHomes are approved by right.

Housing Co-ops:

  • The NCP shall instruct the newly created Saskatchewan Co-operative Development Agency (SCDA) to come up with a plan to transition a portion of the new public housing stock into co-operative housing.

The Funding

The vast majority of the upfront capital for new public and SaskHomes construction will be raised through SaskBonds:

  • SaskBonds will allow residents, pension funds, and institutional investors to invest directly in Saskatchewan Housing, with clear "use of proceeds" tied to public and co-operative housing.
  • Bond proceeds will be used by the SHC to build, buy, and renovate housing, while ongoing operating costs will be supported by rental revenues and statutory funding from the General Revenue Fund.
  • See Policy 21 - Funding - Taxes, Royalties, and METR for more.

Overtime, as the public housing portfolio grows and stabilizes:

  • Net revenues from SHC and related Crowns will help support maintenance, operations, and further expansion, ensuring this is not a one-time burst, but a permanent public housing system.
