The Problem

Education is underfunded, overburdened, under appreciated, and built for the last century.

The Promise

The NCP will fund our education system like never before and ensure that it grows to meet the needs of residents no matter their stage of life.

The Plan

Within 24 months of forming government, the NCP will introduce several bills in a Lifelong Learning Package, that creates:

  • K-12 Funding and Supports.
  • K-12 Curriculum Additions.
  • Post Secondary Funding and Supports.
  • The Saskatchewan Library Authority.
  • The Saskatchewan Distance Learning Centre Expansion.
  • Arts Funding.

K-12 Funding and Supports

  • Per-student funding will be increased to be the highest in Canada, starting at $21,000 per student, indexed to Saskatchewan's CPI inflation annually.
  • Additional student funding will be granted for weighted needs, such as rural, complexity, ELA, and disabilities.
  • Education Assistants and other support roles will be explicitly and statutorily funded based on school size and staffing ratios.
  • In consultation with the Saskatchewan Teacher's Federation (STF), class sizes will be capped and reduced so that educators can give adequate attention and support to all their students.
  • New increased minimum prep times will be introduced with guaranteed hours based on class sizes and grade.
  • The Government of Saskatchewan, in good faith negotiations with the STF, will offer to increase all STF wage bands by 25% in the next contract negotiation, with extra premiums granted to staff in rural and northern settings.
  • Extra Curricular Activities, especially those outside of school hours, will receive new and increased funding that includes educator pay for presiding over such activities. Educators must be fairly compensated for any extra curricular work.
  • Arts programs will receive new and additional funding to ensure students have access to, and education in, a necessary part of the human experience.
  • A new statutory school supplies grant will be given to schools to ensure each classroom has the supplies they need for class work and classroom decoration. No longer will teachers be expected to pay out of pocket.
  • Educators will be offered new bursaries to expand their knowledge and teaching abilities in the following:
    • Indigenous Languages.
    • French.
    • ELA and ESL.
    • and more.
  • New trainings and rules around student behaviours will be introduced as to protect educators and students.
  • Attendance increase schemes or avoidance deterrence will be banned. Children will not be punished for not being able to attend school. Instead new supports and training will be provided to help students, educators, and parents determine the cause of attendance failures.
  • Catholic Schools must:
    • Be required to teach the Provincial Curriculum.
    • Allow students the right to opt out of any and all religious teachings without punishment or assignment / grade failures.
    • Follow the same labour standards and staffing ratios of the public system.
    • Transparently report metrics to the province.
    • Not discriminate students or educators for their religious beliefs, sexual orientations, backgrounds, or any other Human Rights Code protected identity.
  • Private or Independent Schools will no longer receive any public funds 5 years after this section comes into force. Capacity will be increased to transition and accommodate any students from these schools into the public system.
  • Home Schooling will have new restrictions and rules:
    • Educators must get a license from the province in order to homeschool.
    • Educators must submit background checks to the province.
    • Educators must submit annual learning and curricula plans to the Ministry.
    • Educators must follow the provincial curriculum.
    • Students and Educators must meet with Ministry of Education representatives for a quarterly portfolio review.
    • Students must be enrolled, for free, in the Saskatchewan Distance Learning Centre.
      • Grants will be provided for devices as well as internet and electricity bills.
    • Students must complete an annual standardized test for their grade level.
    • The goal is to support quality learning, not punish parents.
    • Enforcement will prioritize support over punishment and escalate slowly.
  • Police in residence will be replace with Indigenous Elders in residence.

K-12 Curriculum Additions:

  • All new curricula must be made in partnership with Indigenous nations, governing bodies, and organizations.
  • The curriculum will be outcomes based, without additional standardized testing.
  • Suggested new subjects, to be taught along side existing subjects as to not overwhelm teachers, include:
    • Civic and Labour History.
    • Media and Digital Literacy.
    • Critical Thinking.
    • Financial Literacy.
    • Consent and Healthy Relationships.
    • Climate and Land-based Learning.
    • Reconciliation and Treaties.
    • Indigenous Languages.

Post Secondary Funding and Supports:

  • Statutory, multi-year funding agreements will be made as to ensure post secondary institutions have a reliable and predictable funding, with a floor to stand on.
  • Operating grants will be set to clear benchmarks around inflation, enrolment growth, population, and agreed program expansions.
  • Tuition will be capped so that it can only increase annually by Saskatchewan's CPI inflation plus a small margin determined in partnership with post secondary sector partners.
  • Tuition Vouchers, which cover actual tuition, fees, books, etc, must be redeemable by the post secondary institution in order to receive any funding by the province.
    • Redemption of vouchers is dependent on seat availability in the selected program.
    • See more about Tuition Vouchers in Policy 11 - The Saskatchewan Century Corps.
  • Post Secondary Institutions will be asked to partner in the creation of training and program specifics for the Saskatchewan Century Corps.
  • Crowns and Ministries will partner with post secondary institutions for training, as well as long-term research on the various new initiatives taken on by the Government of Saskatchewan.
  • A Capacity Expansion Fund will be created:
    • The funding will be tied to that year's demand from SCC Tuition Vouchers and Just Transition retraining, and overall labour market metrics.
    • The Capacity Expansion Fund will fund:
      • New faculty lines in high-demand programs.
      • More clinical instructors in nursing and allied health programs.
      • More lab and shop space for trades and applied programs.
      • Instructional design staff for SaskDLC and other hybrid programs.
      • New research funding.

The Saskatchewan Library Authority (SLA):

  • The SLA will be a new, federated, opt-in group for all libraries in Saskatchewan under one umbrella.
  • For SLA participating libraries, there will be a single, unified Library Card under a unified catalogue province wide.
    • Materials and works where AI-generated content is the primary creative output are not eligible to be part of the catalogue.
  • Participating Libraries must agree to a code of Intellectual Freedom in their collections, meaning:
    • No removal or omissions based on viewpoint.
    • Selection criteria must be transparent.
    • Shelving, both physical and digital, must be age appropriate.
    • Anyone challenging a book's inclusion in the SLA must go through a formal review by an independent panel of librarians.
    • The default bias must be that no books should be banned, with exceptions only for material that violates the criminal code.
  • Physical book delivery will be managed in partnership with the new Saskatchewan Transport Corporation (STC).
    • See more about the STC in Policy 06 - 21st Century Connected Communities.
  • Participating central libraries will receive grants to expand their buildings and services to include maker spaces and equipment lending.
  • Participating libraries will receive grants to purchase connected devices to be lent out for long term periods such as an education semester.
    • Priority will be given to SaskDLC students.
  • The Government of Saskatchewan will negotiate on behalf of participating libraries with publishers to gain better lending terms, such as:
    • Laws to ensure any better agreements found within Canada apply to the SLA.
    • A concurrency floor for digital lending that ensures lending amounts reflect the non-scare nature of digital books.
    • A new, lower price cap for digital and physical material lending.
    • A minimum 15 year or perpetual license on materials.
    • DRM that allows for accessibility features such as screen readers and a minimum 14 days of offline use.
  • Participating libraries will be prohibited from charging any late fees.
  • Data generated by the SLA and participating libraries cannot be given or sold to any outside entity of the Government of Saskatchewan. Library users' data must be protected.
  • A Saskatchewan Open Library program will be introduced that collects out of print, and public domain materials. Such materials will be stored and operated by the SLA and have no limits on digital lending.

The Saskatchewan Distance Learning Centre

  • The Saskatchewan Distance Learning Centre (SaskDLC) will receive a new mandate and new funding to expand their services.
  • Educators from the STF will be recruited and paid their union wages to help create:
    • A free, self-paced, Adult Basic Education program, with official transcripts, open to all.
    • A free, self-paced, Adult Literacy program, with official transcripts, open to all.
    • A free, self-paced, High School upgrading program, with official transcripts, open to all.
    • A free, self-paced, K-12 program, following the provincial curriculum for homeschooled students and educators.
    • Prior Learning Assessment and Recognition programs for new comers and residents.
  • Delivery will be both synchronous and asynchronous for learners to choose their preference, with educators staffed for synchronous delivery of their lessons.
  • Data generated by students, educators, and the SaskDLC is prohibited from being sold, distributed, or used outside the Government of Saskatchewan.
  • Partnerships with the SLA will allow students to get priority in connected device lending so they can participate fully.
  • Tutoring services will be offered to any and all residents using Education Program students in the Universities. These positions would be paid union rates.
  • The SaskDLC will have a goal of being as accessible as possible with various language options, disability first designs, and human made subtitles.

Arts Funding:

  • The Arts are a fundamental part of human life and the province will commit to funding them.
  • An arms length public arts council will be created to distribute and manage the funds.
  • Creation grants and operating grants will be delivered to various artists, festivals, and tourism divisions doing public works.
  • A new tax credit for Film and TV, along with grants, will be created and stack with any federal incentives.
  • A minimum of 20% of Arts funding must be spent outside of Regina and Saskatoon, with extra grants given to small towns.
  • Participating SLA libraries will have regular periodic exhibit spaces for local artists.
  • Materials and works where AI-generated content is the primary creative output are not eligible for funding.

The Funding

Education funding shall come out of the General Revenue fund, with additional funds attained from social based SaskBonds for new capital intensive projects.

What It Means For You

It means a quality education no matter your age or ability.

It means our educators get the pay and recognition they deserve.

It means the dignity in always learning something new.
