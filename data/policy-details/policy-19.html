The Problem

Saskatchewan doesn't have the tools to build its own future.

The Promise

The NCP will bring back manufacturing in a way that serves the people and the goals of Saskatchewan and its future.

The Plan

Within 24 months of forming government, the NCP will introduce The Saskatchewan Manufacturing Corporation Act, which will allow Saskatchewan to start building the future.

  • A new Crown corporation will be made and known as the Saskatchewan Manufacturing Corporation (SMC).
  • The SMC will be mandated to quickly build and operate new modern factories in Saskatchewan using the greenest available technologies and Canadian made supplies where possible.
  • Each factory will be mandated to achieve Net-Zero Operations using 100% renewable power within ten years of operations.
    • This means they must follow the same building standards set out in Policy 06 - 21st Century Connected Communities.
  • Each Factory will be required to generate its own renewable power and operate grid scale batteries to offset the higher grid load the factories require.
  • Each item produced by the SMC must come with a digital material passport that lists exactly what materials are in it, ensuring it can be recycled safely in the future.
  • The SMC will license out the factory designs from factories where this work is already being done around the world, so we can speed up the process and start right away on building.
  • The Board of the SMC must include the main customers of SMC projects, including executives of the Saskatchewan Housing Corporation, SaskEnergy, SaskGrocery, and more.
  • The SMC will operate in the following three subdivisions:
    • SMC Housing:
      • The first priority of the SMC is to build a pre-fab, modular, housing factory to be known as SMC-H1.
      • The housing designs will follow SaskHomes universal designs so they will be uniquely Saskatchewan, ready for the 21st Century, and easily built in a factory and on site.
      • SMC-H1 will focus on building modular, easily connected, volumetric modules, such as bathrooms, or kitchens, and panelized walls, ensuring they meet the new housing code standards.
      • SMC-H1 and beyond will use a mix of cutting edge automation and human skilled trades.
      • Agreements with the Sask Forestry sector will be made to ensure that local resources are used.
      • The Saskatchewan Housing Corporation (SHC) will sign multi-year contracts and demand agreements, ensuring that the demand is there for 24/7 production of SaskHomes.
        • For more about housing see Policy 05 - The Housing Crisis.
    • SMC Energy:
      • SMC will build an assembly factory, to be known as SMC-E1, and it will be tasked to create the heat pumps needed for SaskEnergy to become a Thermal Utility, and the hardware for SaskPower's Virtual Power Plant.
        • See more on SaskEnergy and its Thermal Utility in Policy 06 - 21st Century Connected Communities.
        • See more on SaskPower and its VPP in Policy 15 - Resource and Energy Sovereignty.
      • SMC-E1 and beyond will use a mix of cutting edge automation and human skilled trades.
      • Each item produced by the SMC-E factories must be designed to be fully and easily repairable.
      • SMC-E1 will sell Thermal Utility Boxes that will have all the parts and appliances needed to convert a home to using SaskEnergy's thermal loops.
      • SMC-E1 will also assemble commercial-scale and grid-scale batteries, purchasing the cells off the global market.
    • SMC Agriculture:
      • The SMC will build a regional flour mill so that Saskatchewan Agriculture Board (SAB) wheat and other local wheat makes local flour.
      • The SMC will build small regional abattoirs, so that local farmers don't have to ship to Alberta.
      • The SMC will build oil crushing and bottling factories so that Saskatchewan Canola Oil is made in Saskatchewan.

The Funding

The primary source of funding will come from SaskBonds.

The ongoing operating funds will be paid for through the General Revenue Fund.

Costs will be offset by the sales and processing fees generated by the factories.

What It Means For You

It means our resources meet our needs.

It means a truly local economy.

It means the dignity in doing it ourselves in Saskatchewan.
