The Problem

For generations, settler governments have determined the lives of the Indigenous Peoples of this land. The Government of Saskatchewan has made little real attempt to change that.

The Promise

An NCP government will implement all 94 Calls to Action from the Truth and Reconciliation Commission that fall within Saskatchewan’s jurisdiction and capacity.

An NCP government will recognize and adopt as law the United Nations Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples (UNDRIP), and align provincial laws and policies with it.

The Office of the Reconciliation Commissioner will be established, co-governed and co-developed with Indigenous nations and governing bodies.

The Office of Missing and Murdered Indigenous Women, Girls, and Two-Spirit People (MMIWG2S) will be established, co-governed and co-developed.

The Right of First Refusal will be given to Treaty Nations on all Crown land sales.

September 30 will become a statutory holiday in recognition of the National Day for Truth and Reconciliation.

The Plan

Immediately upon forming government, the NCP will reach out to all Saskatchewan-based Indigenous nations and organizations, and welcome all Indigenous voices, to begin the formal process of building the Consent Protocol.

The Consent Protocol will be a binding requirement on any Government of Saskatchewan decision, stating that before proceeding with any project, policy, or action that might affect Indigenous lands, communities, waters, heritage, culture, language, or livelihoods, the relevant Indigenous governing body or organization must give their free, prior, and informed consent.

This must, where relevant, include:

  • Cost-sharing
  • Revenue-sharing
  • Job opportunities
  • Capacity funding for those affected

Within 24 months, and in partnership with all Saskatchewan Indigenous nations and organizations, a complete and total review of all Government of Saskatchewan activities and initiatives toward completing the 94 Calls to Action will be submitted to the Legislature.

In addition, every Ministry, Crown corporation, agency, and government body must submit a detailed plan on how they will achieve each of the 94 Calls to Action that is possible within its scope and authority.

The new Offices for the Reconciliation Commissioner and for MMIWG2S will be co-developed with Saskatchewan Indigenous nations and governing bodies, who will together bring these offices into being.

The first September 30 after an NCP government is formed will be the first statutory holiday in recognition of the National Day for Truth and Reconciliation in Saskatchewan.

What comes next cannot be dictated. It can only be walked, hand in hand, together.

The Funding

Operating funds for the Offices and core reconciliation work will be provided as statutory, multi-year funding from the General Revenue Fund. This must include capacity funding for Consent Protocols, to ensure Indigenous nations and organizations can fully participate in consultation and consent processes with their own legal, technical, and Elders’ support.

The Saskatchewan Sovereignty Fund Authority will include a dedicated, ring-fenced Reconciliation Fund. This Fund will make annual, controlled transfers to the General Revenue Fund which, by law, may only be used for reconciliation initiatives such as:

  • Land-back and Treaty Land Entitlement
  • Indigenous-led infrastructure
  • Language and cultural revitalization
  • Other priorities co-designed with Indigenous nations and organizations

See Policy 17 – Financial Sovereignty for more.
