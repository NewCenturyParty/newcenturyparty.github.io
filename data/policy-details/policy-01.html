The Problem

Human Dignity has fallen off to the way side of provincial concerns. The current Government has even recently gone so far as to attack the inherent dignity and rights of the most vulnerable children in our province. We cannot move forward if we are stuck in old ways of thinking.

The Promise

An NCP Government will not just simply promise uphold Human Dignity, rather it will bind itself by law so that the primary duty of any Government of Saskatchewan is first and foremost to respect, protect, and uphold Human Dignity.

The Plan

First, within the first twelve months of gaining office, the NCP will put forward The Saskatchewan Human Dignity Act. This Act will make three binding proclamations that will be the basis of all governmental action going forward.

  1. Human dignity is inherent and inviolable.
  2. The primary duty of all provincial authority is to respect, protect, and uphold human dignity.
  3. Indignity can never excuse further indignity.

All enactments and decisions shall, to the extent possible, be ready consistently with this Act. Any later Acts intending to limit or change these declarations must clearly and expressly say so.

Secondly, also within twelve months, the Human Rights Code shall be amended to ensure its interpretation is made specifically to uphold and protect the inherent dignity of all as well as include and strengthen protections based on

  • Neurodiversity
  • Gender Identity and Expression
  • Any Physical or Mental Disability
  • Social Condition
    • Source of Income
    • Employment Status
    • Housing Status

The Funding

Capacity funding will be granted from the General Revenue Fund so that experts in Human Rights Law can expertly bind the Government of Saskatchewan to respecting, upholding, and protecting Human Dignity for all, as well as add new discrimination protections in the Human Rights Code.

What It Means For You

This means no more wondering if your government cares about you, because by law it must. And if the government ever fails to uphold, respect, or protect your dignity, you have the right to take us to court.

No matter your identity, your background, what your story is, you have the right to live with dignity.
