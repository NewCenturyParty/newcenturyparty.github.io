The Problem

Workers are tied up and invested in a dying industry.

The Promise

The NCP will be there to support workers ready to join a 21st Century economy.

The Plan

Within 12 months of forming government, the NCP will introduce The Saskatchewan Just Transition Act.

This Act will enable workers in specified Oil and Gas NAICS code industries (extraction, pipelines, support, etc), including related dependent contractors and on-site service workers who physically work over 50% of hours in the specified Oil and Gas industries, to choose three different pathways out of the industry.

To be eligible, workers must have been working in the specified Oil and Gas NAICS code industries for 12 of the last 24 months, have the legal right to live or work in Saskatchewan, and commit to residing and working in Saskatchewan during these pathways. Workers can only apply once.

The Just Transition Office (JTO) shall be established to act as the program manager for these pathways.

These pathways will sunset after 10 years.

Retire:

  • For those who have served their time and are ready to take their well deserved rest, a pension bridge will be offered to allow for early retirement.
  • The province will pay the unreduced expected pension for the individual for up to 6 years until they qualify for their OAS/CPP or their plan's full unreduced pension.
  • You must live and retire in Saskatchewan to qualify.

Retrain:

  • For those who want to receive education in new priority sectors, a full scholarship (tuition, fees, books, tools, bus pass) will be offered for any post-secondary or vocational training done in Saskatchewan, provided they meet satisfactory academic progress.
    • Priority sectors will be determined by regulations, but will be focused on the new energy economy.
  • In addition, 50% of the worker's Average Weekly Earnings (AWE) from their old job will be paid as a monthly stipend while the worker is in full-time schooling for up to 48 months.
    • AWE is calculated based off CRA records of the average earnings over the prior highest-earning 26 of the last 52 weeks and is capped at the year's maximum pensionable earnings (YMPE) and the year's additional maximum pensionable earnings (YAMPE).
  • A monthly childcare stipend will also be offered to those with children under the age of 13 while the worker is in full-time schooling for up to 48 months.

Redeploy:

  • For those who are ready to move to the new economy right away, they will enter a priority hiring pool created, by SaskJobs, and will receive a suitable offer in no more than 45 days.
  • If the SCC wage, or a new private sector job's wage, is less than what the worker was earning in their previous job, wage insurance will be offered for up to 48 months.
  • Wage insurance will start coverage at 50% of the difference between the new job's wage and the old job's AWE at the start, and gradually decline to 0% by the end of 48 months.
  • Relocation grants will be offered and vary depending on distance and family size to help pay for the move and the first three months of new housing.
    • Only Saskatchewan residents with proof of a Saskatchewan address qualify.
  • A monthly childcare stipend will also be offered to those with children under the age of 13 while the worker is in full-time employment for up to 48 months.

Additional One-Time Supports:

  • A transition bonus of $3000.
    • Clawbacks will occur if the worker exits the Just Transition process within 90 days without cause.
  • Family Mental Health Coverage.
    • Until the full Universal Health policy is implemented, households can receive $5000 for counselling.
  • Health Benefits Bridge.
    • Until the full Universal Health policy is implemented, households will receive provincially provided extended health benefits to cover the sectors listed under Universal Health.
      • See more in policy 03 - Universal Health

The Just Transition Office

  • The Just Transition Office (JTO) will handle the applications and payment disbursements for these pathways, and work with other organizations like SaskJobs to place people into new jobs.
  • The JTO will also prevent fraud and misuse by:
    • Conducting random audits.
    • Compelling records.
    • Preventing duplicate applications.
    • Clawing back benefits for those who leave the new jobs within 12 months.
  • Case managers will be hired and capped at 60 applicants to ensure personal and timely service.

The Funding

In specified Oil and Gas NAICS codes industries, businesses will pay a 1.5% Just Transition surcharge on all annual Saskatchewan revenues. This surcharge will be paid into the Saskatchewan Sovereignty Fund's Resilience Fund.

An annual endowment from the Resilience Fund of the Saskatchewan Sovereignty Fund will be paid to the General Revenue Fund, which by law must be used for the above stated Just Transition pathways and the Just Transition Office.

See more in policy 17 - Financial Sovereignty.
