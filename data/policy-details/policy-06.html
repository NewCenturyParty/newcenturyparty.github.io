The Problem

Cities are held back by old ways of thinking that are not compatible with Human Thriving.

Our infrastructure is nearing its replacement age.

There are no reliable transit options between communities across the province.

The Promise

An NCP Government will ensure our cities meet the standards of the 21st Century and that transportation between communities is reliable, frequent, and cheap.

The Plan

21st Century Communities

Within 12 months of forming government, the NCP shall introduce amendments to:

  • The SaskEnergy Act
  • The Saskatchewan Telecommunications Act.
  • The Municipalities Act
  • The Construction Codes Act
  • The Highways and Transportation Act

SaskEnergy - From Gas Utility to Thermal Utility

  • A new mandate will be given to SaskEnergy to transition from a Natural Gas Utility to a Thermal Utility, starting in Priority Housing Zones.
    • See Policy 05 - Housing Crisis for more on Priority Housing Zones.
  • The goal will be not to replace natural gas home heating, but rather to
    • Keep gas heating as an emergency backstop so that no matter what your house stays warm.
    • Make the primary heating and cooling system an in home, hydronic heat pump system connected to 5th generation ambient loops.
  • A 5th generation ambient loop is a series of deep underground pipes circulating a water solution throughout your neighbourhood to connect to your home.
    • The water runs at a stable ground temperature and is buried deep enough that it won't freeze in the winter.
    • In-home heat pumps then move heat from the loop into the house when its cold, and the moves the heat from the house back into the loop when its hot, providing highly-efficient heating and cooling.
    • These systems can connect to existing ductwork in homes easily, and there are many alternatives for those without ductwork.
  • SaskEnergy will explore additional heat sources for ambient loops such as:
    • Vertical geothermal boreholes beneath large public spaces.
    • Other geothermal power and heat capture opportunities.
  • No new gas hookups will be permitted after 15 years from the amendment coming into force.
  • By law the thermal tariff on the household must be cheaper over its lifecycle than what that household was paying for natural gas.

SaskTel - Connected and Sovereign

  • SaskTel must ensure affordable fibre optic internet is available in every household in Saskatchewan.
    • The current roll out must be completed within five years of the amendment coming into force.
  • SaskTel must create an enterprise grade, public facing, renewed email service for use by Government and the public at large.
    • This service will optional and free for the public, but mandatory and paid for Government use.
    • This service must be ultra private and secure, treating email with the same protections physical mail receives.
    • See more in policy 16 - Digital Sovereignty

Municipalities Act - Human Centred Cities

  • Municipalities may not approve gated communities or developments that restrict public movement between neighbourhoods. The free passage throughout the city must always be preserved.
  • Cities must be designed with Humans and Human Dignity first, not only for cars.
  • All streets must gradually transition to include wide, protected bike paths.
  • Trams and streetcars must be allowed as-of-right in street design and planning.
  • The municipalities must work and coordinate with SaskEnergy, SaskPower, and SaskTel to implement a Dig Once policy for underground infrastructure.
  • Single Family Zoning must be phased out within 10 years.
  • Municipalities must create Priority Housing Zones around priority areas designated by the province (downtowns, transit corridors, schools, main streets, hospitals), with as-of-right approvals for SaskHomes and Provincial Standard Plans
    • See more in policy 05 - Housing Crisis

Grants for 21st Century Communities

  • Grants will be provided to municipalities that adopt and implement 21st century standards, including:
    • Transit Improvements
      • Longer service hours.
      • More frequent and reliable service.
      • Extra grants for those that build new trams / street cars.
    • 20 minute Walkable Cities
      • Designing networks and communities so that 90% of residents are within a 20 minute walk of the following key amenities:
        • Grocery Stores.
        • Primary Care.
        • Primary and Secondary School.
        • Childcare.
        • Parks and Green Spaces.
        • Frequent Transit stops.
        • Public Libraries and Maker Spaces.
    • Street Standards
      • Protected Bike Lanes wherever possible.
      • Traffic Calming Street Designs.
      • Close school zone streets to through-traffic during certain hours.
      • Tree Canopy Coverage targets.
      • Safe crossings and intersection redesigns.
    • Parking Reform
      • Remove Parking minimums in new developments
    • Zoning Reform
      • Abolish Single Family Zoning.
      • Allow for mixed use development.
      • Allow missing middle housing.
      • Allow storefront main floors with housing units above.
    • Land Value Tax
      • Province will enable LVT as an option by law.
      • LVT will be an option municipalities can choose to adopt, and will not be imposed on any municipality without their explicit approval.
      • 10 year transition period.
      • Deferrals for low income households.
      • Must include home owner protections.
      • Phases in as regular Property Taxes phase out where adopted.

21st Century Construction Codes:

  • New building codes will be written to ensure that our homes are ready for anything the 21st Century Brings. This includes:
    • Meet Passive House level or equivalent efficiency
    • Be net-zero ready and climate resilient.
    • Be super insulated and weatherized inside and out.
    • Provide clean indoor air, designed for wildfire smoke and respiratory pandemics with air exchange and filtration that approach or exceed hospital levels.
    • Universally Accessible with
      • Wide spaces for mobility devices
      • Braille and tactile features where appropriate
      • Both auditory and visual alarms
    • Be prewired and structurally ready for decentralized energy options like solar panels and battery storage.
    • Provide abundant natural light.
    • Building materials that are
      • Non-toxic.
      • Fire resistant.
      • Documented via Material Passports so we know what was used, where, and when it needs upgrading.
    • Include water systems that are efficient and allow for grey water recycling where feasible.
    • All wiring must be child-safe yet accessible, so it can be easily replaced and upgraded.
    • Ensure a certain level of acoustic comfort for every unit.

Dig Once:

  • All digging needed for the new infrastructure mentioned must be coordinated to follow a Dig Once policy as to minimize the disturbance to the public.

Connecting the Communities

The Highways and Transportation Act - Reviving the STC

  • A new crown corp named the Saskatchewan Transport Company (STC) will be established.
  • The STC is reimagined from just purely transit company into being a public logistics, package and freight, and personal transport company.
  • The service will connect any community of over 1000 people on a regular AM / PM basis, with more service provided for heavily used routes.
  • The buses will be custom ordered as to ensure large storage capacity, with feasibility studies conducted to explore dividing the bus into sections for secure cold storage, freight, and passengers because we should be moving people and supplies at the same time.
  • Routes will end at STC Depots around the province. Each depot will include:
    • Timed route transfers.
    • A unified ticketing service (Physical and Digital).
    • Cold chain storage.
    • Package pick up and drop off.
  • The expected primary customers of this new STC are:
    • The Saskatchewan public looking for transit between their communities.
    • SaskGrocery to deliver groceries and supplies between stores across the province.
    • Pharmacies to deliver prescriptions across the province.
    • Business or individuals looking to ship items or freight inter-provincially.
  • A project for high-speed rail line between Regina and Saskatoon will be established with serious planning, corridor protection, and feasibility work beginning within 12 months.

The Funding

The vast majority of the upfront capital for new public infrastructure will be raised through SaskBonds:

  • SaskBonds will allow residents, pension funds, and institutional investors to invest directly in Saskatchewan Infrastructure, with clear "use of proceeds" tied to the thermal utility setups, new builds, fibre installs, STC buses procured, and more.

  • Bond proceeds will be used by SaskEnergy, SaskPower, and SaskTel to complete their new and expanded mandates, with ongoing operating costs supported by things like thermal tariffs, electricity rates, and internet service revenues.

  • Net revenues over time will help support maintenance, operations, and further expansion as need and demand grows.
