The Problem

Our home is actively being destroyed by the fossil fuel industry, who knows, and has always known, exactly what they are doing.

The Promise

The NCP will immediately do everything in its power, be it regulatory, fiscal, or legal, to tackle the climate crisis and hold to account the people in power responsible.

The Plan

Immediately upon forming government, the NCP will submit an Order-in-Council ending fossil fuel subsidies, royalty holidays, grants, and promotion.

Within 6 months of forming government, the NCP will introduce the Saskatchewan Climate Crisis Act to give the right to a health environment to all Saskatchewan residents, legislate the end of fossil fuel subsidies, enforce the clean up and remediation of all existing and non-active fossil fuel sites, create the Saskatchewan Environmental Economics Agency (SEEA), and obligate the following:

Within 36 month of forming government, the NCP will bring forward the largest, most comprehensive lawsuit in the history of this province, suing industry and Ottawa for willful and extraordinary, damages, deception, and neglect.

The Saskatchewan Climate Crisis Act:

  • Every Saskatchewan Resident has the right to a healthy environment, as determined by scientific consensus. Any breach of that is a violation of their rights, dignity, and Saskatchewan law.
  • Following the Order-in-Council, by law no provincial instrument, be it tax credit, grant, royalty adjustment, Crown loan, or infrastructure subsidy, may be used to expand or continue fossil fuel extraction, processing, or transport.
  • The Saskatchewan Environmental Economics Agency (SEEA) will be established with a mandate to provide an accurate and full economic breakdowns of all the externalities related to fossil fuels, the damages done to the Saskatchewan environment, Saskatchewan resident's health, as well as the expected costs of remediation now and into the future as climate change worsens.
  • The SEEA will have the power to compel any and all records, testimony, or proprietary data from operators regarding emissions, reservoir health, and liabilities from any and all fossil fuel sector companies or operators operating in Saskatchewan.
  • The SEEA must provide complete reports on all costs within 30 months of coming into force.
  • Any and all new fossil fuel extraction, or significant modification or expansion of existing sites, must provide an upfront deposit of 100% of the estimated total clean up costs, as determined by SEEA, before any work may start.
  • A levy will be charged on all liable parties for abandoned or orphaned fossil fuel sites, that can be refunded should the site be cleaned up by the liable company, or companies, within a set timeframe and deemed adequately done by an independent climate body. If the site is not cleaned up by the liable company or companies within a set timeframe, the levy is forfeited to the province to fund the remediation.
  • Rules of Civil Procedure:
    • Burden of Proof:
      • If the province can demonstrate that a defendant (a) produced or promoted fossil fuels and (b) knew or ought to have known about the climate risks, it is presumed that their actions contributed to climate damages in Saskatchewan.
      • The burden then shifts entirely to the defendant to prove that their specific emissions did not contribute to the damage.
    • Statistical and Aggregate Evidence Admissibility:
      • The use of epidemiological, sociological, and climate-attribution data to prove damages is explicitly permitted.
      • Individual and specific litres of gas or other fossil product need not be proven to cause specific damages, rather such fossil product can be proven to increase the statistical probability and or severity of climate damages such as wildfires, droughts, and climate related health issues in the province.
    • Liability by Market Share:
      • Because we share one planet, and pollution in one place may damage the environment of another place in the world, liability will be established based on the defendant's share of the global market for fossil fuels during the period of deception.
      • If a company is responsible for 2% of historic global emissions, they are thus liable for 2% of Saskatchewans total climate damages.
      • This liability is "Joint and Several" meaning if one defendant goes bankrupt, the now missing liability goes to all other fossil fuel companies in the industry.
    • Retroactive Application:
      • This Act applies retroactively to conduct dating back to the point where industry knowledge of climate change can be proven.
    • Asset Forfeiture for Non-Payment:
      • The Act will mandate that failure to satisfy a court judgment results in the immediate seizure of any and all assets located within the province to satisfy the debt.
  • Within 36 months or earlier if SEEA certifies readiness, the Government of Saskatchewan must bring forth a comprehensive lawsuit against the fossil fuel sector and Ottawa for the damages they have created and knowingly allowed.
