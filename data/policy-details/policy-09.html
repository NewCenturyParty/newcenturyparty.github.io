The Problem

People are living to work, not working to live, and the work is unfair, unsatisfying, and unbalanced.

The Promise

The NCP will bring labour into the 21st Century through a new Worker Bill of Rights, Apprenticeship reforms, and entities to make sure it sticks.

The Plan

Within 12 months of forming government, the NCP will introduce bills that create:

  • Apprenticeship Expansion.

  • The Fair Work Authority

  • SaskJobs as an active labour market maker.

  • A Workers’ Bill of Rights.

Apprenticeships:

  • The government shall work with the Saskatchewan Apprenticeship and Trade Certification Commission (SATCC) to grow apprenticeship spots and infrastructure.
  • When trades or ROAs are present, procurements and grants above a certain capital threshold, set by regulation, must have a certain number of apprenticeship hours, again set by regulation, aiming for over 25% of labour hours as certified apprenticeship hours for all capital projects over $250,000.
  • Licensing / permitting conditions in sectors chosen by regulation shall be required to have Apprenticeship/Trainee Participation Plans.
  • The [[The Apprenticeship and Trade Certification Act, 2019]] will be amended to create Registered Occupational Apprenticeships (ROAs) by designating the following sectors as able to have apprenticeships.
    • Care.
    • Digital / Tech.
    • Logistics.
    • Agriculture / Aquaculture.
    • Dental.
    • and more.
  • Official SATCC logbooks will be used to capture hours and exams will be delivered by Polytech and the public universities in Saskatchewan.
  • The government shall work with SATCC to provide a free online portal to log hours / track times easily.
  • Tax credits will be given to employers who increase or open new apprenticeship placements or spots.

The Fair Work Authority (FWA):

  • A new agency of the Government of Saskatchewan, to be known as the Fair Work Authority (FWA), working under the Ministry of Labour Relations and Workplace Safety, must be created to inspect and enforce the new fair labour rules.
  • The Fair Work Authority (FWA) will have the power to:
    • Enter workplaces, announced or unannounced.
    • Compel any and all relevant records.
    • Issue compliance orders.
    • Issue Administrative Monetary Penalties.
    • Administer monthly payroll credits.
    • Administer tax credits for employers who open up new apprenticeship placements and spots.
    • Take complaints, anonymous or public, from individuals or organizations.
    • Fine and garnish entities for non compliance.

SaskJobs:

  • SaskJobs will be transformed under a new mandate to become an active labour-market operator that places people quickly into quality jobs or paid training.
  • All jobs in Saskatchewan must be posted on SaskJobs for 7-14 days by default.
    • Exceptions granted by the Fair Work Authority for union/internal postings, emergency hires, and hard-to-fill roles with concurrent hiring.
  • Career Navigators will be hired to manage caseloads with outcome targets.
  • Training vouchers and wage subsidies will be negotiated with various employers so workers can get paid, on the job, training fast.
    • Employers with an average retention rate of six months will not qualify.
  • In critical occupations, determined by regulation, retention bonuses will be paid to workers who stay in placed occupations for more than 6 months, with an additional bonus for those staying over 12 months.
  • SaskJobs will produce material on worker rights that must be placed in worksites.
  • SaskJobs will partner with Saskatchewan's ministries and crown corporations to provide workers with opportunities to serve the public.

Workers’ Bill of Rights:

  • Minimum Wage:
    • Increased to $25 / hr and indexed to Saskatchewan's CPI general inflation each year.
  • Full Time Hours:
    • All provincially regulated sectors will have the defined full time hours be reduced from 40 hours per week to 30 hours per week, with part-time work considered to be under 30 hours / week.
    • Suggested scheduling options:
      • 5 Day Week with 6 Hour Days
      • 4 Day Week with 7.5 Hour Days
      • 3 Day Week with 10 Hour Days
    • Alternative Schedules:
      • Employers may apply through the Fair Work Authority (FWA) for a renewable 2 year Service-Equivalency Permit only if:
      • Over a 4 week cycle, the average is less than or equal to 30 hours / week.
      • The service levels are maintained or improved.
      • Pay protections are met.
      • The relevant union agrees via majority vote.
      • 66% of the workers affected vote in favour through a secret ballot run by the labour board.
      • Labour data is publicly published quarterly.
      • Any renewal is agreed to by the relevant union and over 75% of affected workers.
      • Non compliance will result in revocation of permit.
    • Pay Protection
      • The conversion to 30-hour standard shall not reduce any employee's weekly, bi-weekly, or monthly base pay.
      • Employers must adjust hourly rates to ensure base pay of every worker is no lower than pre-Act.
        • Base pay is calculated as the average hourly earnings of an employee over the prior 26 weeks, excluding weeks with unpaid leave, and including all premium pay.
      • For example:
        • 40 hours / week x $20.00 / hour = $800.00 / week
        • 30 hours / week x $26.67 / hour = $800.10 / week
      • Benefits, such as extended health or pensions, cannot be reduce in actuarial value or employer cost share due to the full time hours standards change.
    • Employers cannot discriminate or reduce coverage or delivery of benefit packages or compensation based on amount of hours worked. All employees are entitled to the full benefits offered by employers regardless of full or part time status.
    • Employers may not reduce scheduled hours below the pre-Act average for 12 months without Fair Work Authority approval citing bona fide downturn.
  • Overtime:
    • Workers are entitled to the greater of weekly or daily overtime.
    • Weekly:
      • 1.5x after 30 Hours
      • 2.0x after 40 Hours
      • 2.5x after 50 hours
    • Daily (depending on schedule):
      • if 5 Day Week with 6 Hour Days
        • 1.5 x base pay on hours worked after 6 hours
        • 2.0 x base pay on hours worked after 10 hours
      • if 4 Day Week with 7.5 Hour Days
        • 1.5 x base pay on hours worked after 7.5 hours
        • 2.0 x base pay on hours worked after 10 hours
      • if 3 Day Week with 10 hour Days
        • 2.0 x base pay on hours worked after 10 hours
        • 2.5 x base pay on hours worked after 12 hours
  • Rest:
    • Mandatory 11 consecutive hours off between shifts.
      • Exceptions will be granted for emergency services, and for any worker who requests otherwise in writing.
      • This cannot be requested more than once every 48 hours.
      • Employees who are exempted must be compensated with premium pay.
    • Breaks
      • 1 x 20 min paid break earned after every 2.5 hours.
      • 1 x 30 min paid meal break of 30 minutes if shifts exceeds 5.5 hours.
      • Breaks cannot be waived or combined.
      • Breaks are duty free and employers must ensure coverage during these breaks.
    • Weekends
      • Minimum 48 hours consecutive hours off per 7 day period.
      • 24 hour weekly rest with permit from Fair Work Authority
  • Vacation:
    • Vacation pay must be 8%/10%/12% of the employees gross earnings depending on amount of yearly vacation time earned.
    • Based on years of service, the mandated amount of vacation time is the following:
      • 4 weeks of vacation after 1 year of service.
      • 5 weeks of vacation after 3 years of service.
      • 6 weeks of vacation after 6 years of service.
    • Employees must have the option to cash out their vacation time by request.
    • Vacation can be carried over year over year at a maximum of 4 weeks per year.
  • Sick Days:
    • After 30 days of service, all employees are entitled to:
      • Accrue 1.25 days per month of paid, job protected sick days.
        • First five days are front-loaded past thirty days.
        • 7.5 days worth of wages is paid by employer.
        • 7.5 days worth of wages is paid by Province.
          • Paid as monthly payroll credit, audited quarterly to prevent fraud.
      • Sick notes can only be required after 3 consecutive days missed or 6 days within a 14 day period.
  • Termination:
    • Probation cannot be longer than 3 months.
    • Severance pay is mandatory for all employees who have served for over 1 year.
      • 1.5 weeks per year service up to a maximum of 52 weeks.
      • Cannot be offset with EI or earned wages.
    • Any Health benefits must continue for 8 weeks post termination.
    • Employees continuing service after probations can only be let go for just cause, with the burden on the employer to prove via progressive discipline measures.
    • Mass Layoff Protection for solvent employers:
      • In formal insolvency situations, BIA/CCAA govern.
      • Severance begins after last day of employment with exceptions for unforeseeable business circumstances, subject to FWA review.
      • WARN-style notice.
        • 90 days (20-199 workers)
        • 120 days (200-499 workers)
        • 180 days (500+ workers)
  • Equal Pay:
    • Employees in the same or substantially similar roles, defined by regulations, must be paid equally, regardless if they are full-time or part-time, temp or contract.
    • Absolutely no discrimination based on gender, or any other marginalization is allowed.
    • Standard lawful differentials based on seniority, merit, quantity/quality of output will be allowed.
  • Schedules:
    • Employers must provide 14 schedule days at a time, 14 days before the scheduled period begins.
    • Employees must be given 72 hours notice of any changes.
    • Any shifts changed without 72 hours notice must come with premium pay of an additional +25% of base wage per hour worked of the affected shift(s).
  • Wage Theft:
    • Wage theft complaints will be heard and investigated by the Fair Work Authority (FWA).
    • Employers will have 14 days to correct any substantiated claims of wage theft.
    • Past the 14 days, proven wage theft will be fined treble the amount of damages, with interest accruing immediately after those 14 days, and possible disbarment from provincial contracts, provincial grant eligibility, and potential loss of business license.
    • The liability of wage theft will include both the active manager of the enterprise and the owners of the enterprise.
    • Tips are protected wages that belong strictly to the employees, and must be given to the employees within a reasonable timeframe, and never used for business expenses or operations.
    • Upon employee request, paper copies of any and all paystubs they should have received during their time of employment must be freely available to them for the following three years past their last date of employment.
  • Gig Protections:
    • Workers are presumed to be employed by their hiring entity unless the hiring entity can prove all three:
      • A - The worker is free from the control and direction of the hiring entity in the performance of the work and in fact;
      • B - The worker performs work that is outside the usual course of the hiring entity's business; and
      • C - The worker is customarily engaged in an independently established trade, occupation, or business of the same nature as that involved in the work performed.
    • Platforms and contractors must provide their workers with:
      • EI deductions.
      • CPP deductions.
      • WCB insurance deductions.
      • Expense (vehicle, device, data, insurance) reimbursement separate from wages.
      • An earnings floor no less than minimum wage tied to engaged time.
  • Transparency:
    • All jobs must be posted on SaskJobs:
      • Posting must include the expected range of hourly earnings as well as expected annual earnings at full time.
    • In provincially regulated sectors, any portion of an employment contract that stipulates a non-compete clause is void with narrow exceptions for sale-of-business executive carve-outs to avoid collateral litigation.
    • In provincially regulated sectors, new employment contracts are strictly prohibited from including any non-compete clauses.
    • Any section of a contract forcing arbitration on employees or any class action waiver is void.
    • In provincially regulated sectors, any portion of new or existing contracts containing a ban on salary-history discussion or wage secrecy clauses are void.
  • Benefits Protections:
    • The employer costs of the Universal Health Levy is strictly prohibited from being passed on to the employee.
  • Remote Work:
    • Any employee may request and receive work from home accommodation for all jobs that can reasonably be done from home, as determined by the Fair Work Authority.
    • Workers must have a percentage, determined by regulations, of their costs of working from home reimbursed by their employer, including:
      • Internet.
      • Electricity.
      • Internet.
      • Equipment not provided by the employer (desk, chair, etc).
    • Employees have the right to disconnect and not be contacted about work matters after scheduled work hours.
      • Contact includes any calls, texts, emails, or app pings.
      • Exceptions will be granted for emergency services.
      • If contacted about work after work hours, the employee is entitled to on the job pay of a minimum of 2 hours.
      • On call workers must receive a stipend and call-in minimum.
  • Safety:
    • Extreme weather events must be accommodated for by the employer, ensuring:
      • Paid cool-down, warm-up, clear air breaks are provided.
      • PPE (such as n95 masks), AC and or Heaters are provided.
    • All workers have the right to refuse unsafe work and can directly appeal to the Fair Work Authority (FWA) should they feel the work they are being asked to do is unsafe. The FWA will investigate and issue guidance following such an appeal.
  • Transition Grants:
    • For up to 24 months following the coming into force of this Worker Bill of Rights, businesses can apply for transition grants to help with costs, so long as they attest to using those grants to fund wage or benefit adjustments, not unrelated costs.
      • Small Businesses / Co-ops
        • Up to $2000 / employee
        • Maximum of 30 employees.
      • Medium Businesses / Co-ops
        • Up to $1500 / employee
        • Maximum of 30 employees.
