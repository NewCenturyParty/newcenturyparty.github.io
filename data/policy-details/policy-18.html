The Problem

People don't believe in democracy as much as they used to because of shady backroom deals, and an electoral system that divides city and rural voters against each other.

The Promise

The NCP will restore the foundations of democracy within this province, and ensure that everyone's voice is heard.

The Plan

Within 12 months of forming government, the NCP will introduce The Saskatchewan Democratic Renewal Act.

This Act will be split into the following four sections:

  • Electoral Reform (MMP).
  • Lobbying Reform (The Sunshine Initiative).
  • Campaign Finance Reform (No Outside Influence).
  • Transparency (CodeShare Civics).

Electoral Reform:

  • Voting in Saskatchewan will be mandatory.
    • Fines will be levied at no more than $25.
    • Fines will be waived provided there is reasonable justification made to the court.
  • Digital Voting machines will be prohibited. Saskatchewan will exclusively use paper ballots for voting, counted without the use of digital vote machines.
  • The voting age in Saskatchewan will be lowed to 16 years of age.
  • Voting periods will be extended.
    • Instead of a singular election day, there will be an election week, with votes totalled on the last day of that week.
    • Early voting periods will be extended as well.
  • Independents' Protections:
    • If two or more independent MLAs, Regional or Local, are elected, an Independent Caucus of the Saskatchewan Legislature will be formed.
    • The Independent Caucus will granted a secretariat and legally guaranteed the same per-member funding parity as with registered political parties, ensuring they have equal access to research, legal, and administrative staff.
    • The Independent Caucus or its secretariat will have no authority to whip votes.
  • The Standing Orders of the Legislature will be changed so that any non budgetary proposal in the Legislature requires an Absolute Majority of seats voting in favour to pass.
    • Budgets will continue to pass through a simple majority vote in favour.
  • Saskatchewan will adopt a Mixed Member Proportional (MMP) electoral system.
    • The total number of seats in the Saskatchewan Legislature will increase to 100.
      • 55 of those seats will be for Local MLAs.
      • 45 of those seats will be for Regional MLAs.
        • These seats are proportionately distributed to parties, and any independents, based on Regional vote share earned.
        • To be eligible for any Regional MLA seats, a party, or independents, or none of the above, must earn over 5% of that region's total vote.
          • Independents appearing on the Regional vote will be grouped together for the purposes of calculating the 5% threshold and seat allocation.
        • Regions to represent residents will be fairly made by Elections Saskatchewan to split up the province into multiple, equally, represented zones based on population and location.
      • Proportionality will be achieved using the Sainte-Laguë / Webster Method to calculate seat distribution.
      • Overhang seats, resulting from more local representatives being elected than their party is proportionally entitled to, will result in no more than 20 additional seats being distributed to the parties, or independents, until proportionality is achieved, or seats run out.
        • If proportionality still cannot be achieved, the minimal deviation outcome prevails and a written report must be released detailing the decision within 30 days.
      • There will be one ballot with two votes, with an open list created by Elections Saskatchewan.
        • The first vote will be for your local MLA. Just like now it will list a candidate and their party affiliation or independent status.
        • The second vote will be for both your regional MLA and their listed party, or independent status.
          • This vote will be used to calculate the proportional distribution percentage of seats in the Legislature, and which Candidates from the parties, or the independent's group, to place in those seats.
        • Each vote will list candidates alongside a "none of the above" (NOTA) option.
          • If NOTA wins a Local Riding or a plurality of a Regional Riding, the election is considered void, and a new by-election must be run with all new candidates within 90 days.
            • The candidates from the previous election are barred from running in the by-election, respecting the results of residents already choosing none of them.
          • If NOTA wins over 5% of the total vote case in a region, NOTA wins the proportional amount of seats entitled to them by vote share percentage earned.
            • NOTA seats will remain empty until the next election.

    Lobbying Reform (The Sunshine Initiative):

    • The Conflict of Interest Commissioner will be granted expanded powers with additional funding and resources to ensure the following lobbying rules are strictly enforced.
    • Consultant Lobbying, also known as third-party lobbying, will be banned in Saskatchewan, and paying for such services will be prohibited.
      • Those who wish to lobby the government must do so through their own senior officers or permanent employees.
      • No more selling access.
    • A "cool-off" period will be enacted so that public servants are barred from all lobbying activities for no less than five years after they leave public service.
    • The Sunshine Initiative:
      • Ministers, MLAs, Deputy Ministers, Assistant Deputy Ministers, and all other public servants will be prohibited from taking any and all off record lobbying meetings.
      • Before any official lobbying can take place, the lobbyist must register in a public database with the Province, who will keep an active Lobbyist Registry.
      • Unofficial lobbying is prohibited, and any attempts to lobby a public servant must be reported immediately to the Speaker of the Legislative Assembly, who must bring the issue forward to the Legislature.
        • Failure to report may result in suspension from office.
      • All meetings with lobbyists must:
        • Be publicly scheduled 24 hours in advanced.
        • Be recorded with both high quality audio and video with a separate redundant audio recording.
        • Have all materials presented, the recordings, and a summary of what was discussed posted online and publicly available within 48 hours.
      • Any casual, unscheduled meetings between public servants and lobbyists where government business is discussed must be logged with a summary of what was discussed and publicly posted online.

    Campaign Finance Reform (No Outside Influence):

    • Donations:
      • Donations from Unions or Corporations or other third-parties will be prohibited.
      • Donations will only be allowed from residents of Saskatchewan who have valid Saskatchewan government ID. Foreign or outside donation of any kind is strictly prohibited.
      • Residents may donate to political parties, organizations, Saskatchewan based Political Action Committees (PACs), or third-party political advertisers.
      • Donations will be limited to a Global Aggregate Contribution of $1200 per year per resident, indexed to inflation.
        • This means that $1200 is the total amount any individual in Saskatchewan can donate to any combination of Registered Political Parties, Constituency Associations, Candidates, Nomination Contestants, and Registered Third Party Advertisers (PACs).
      • All donations must be publicly reported and posted online within 48 hours by the receiver.
      • In-Kind contributions are equally capped to the Global Aggregate Contribution of $1200.
        • This means gifts, or hospitality stays, or any other services that might be construed as donation of value cannot pass the donor's $1200 annual limit.
    • Advertising:
      • During the Election Period, when the writ is dropped, Third Party Advertisers are subject to a strict spending limit of $150,000.
      • The use of brokered personal data to create and target political advertising is strictly prohibited.
      • Any political advertising that is intentionally targeted to an audience within Saskatchewan, or deals with topics of Saskatchewan policy, falls under the jurisdiction of the Saskatchewan Democratic Renewal Act.
      • Third-party political groups or advertisers can only spend money on Saskatchewan targeted political advertising via donations from Saskatchewan residents, and, as stated above, those donations cannot exceed $1200.
      • All political ads, including digital ads, must be registered with Elections Saskatchewan 24 hours before posting / airing with the registration made public within 48 hours.
        • Every ad must disclose who paid for the ad, how much, and who is the intended audience / target.
      • Digital Platforms and Broadcasters are prohibited from accepting political advertising payments for Saskatchewan target audiences from unregistered entities.
      • No political ads may be shown 48 hours before election day(s).
    • Per-Vote Subsidy:
      • Parties and winning Independents will receive a small stipend for every vote they received in the previous election.

    CodeShare Civics:

    • CodeShare Civics is a method of participatory democracy that allows residents to see, and even propose changes to, the law, treating it like an open source project.
    • An online, version-controlled, repository (repo), acting as a canonical mirror to the Saskatchewan Gazette, will be created to store and publicly display all non-private Government of Saskatchewan information.
      • This repo will work using a version control software known as git, hosted on sovereign self-hosted platform similar to GitHub or GitLabs on servers in Saskatchewan, to track all changes made to all documents, including laws, showing which MLA or public servant, wrote it, which party they belonged to if applicable, and the date it was added, changed, or proposed.
    • Visually, for residents, it will be in a "wiki" style where all documents, be it a policy, regulation, or full statute, are easily searchable and readable, with relevant information on the document included on the side and in inline with the text, including links to other relevant documents. Each Page would have:
      • A regular view, which would show just the document and relevant information, like who wrote it, when it was published, and options to see previous versions.
      • A Diff view, which would show a side by side of the current and previous version(s) of the document, highlighting each addition in green, and highlighting each removal in red, ensuring anyone can easily see what changed, who changed it, when it was changed, and a reason included with the change.
      • A Suggested Changes view will be available for all to see and "like" or "support" the resident proposed changes to the document.
      • A Discussion page for each document, where the public can comment on the document or proposed changes with others.
      • A File view which would show the entire repository file system, for those who find that style more suitable.
    • An edit or "Suggest Change" button will be on all pages, allowing verified residents to suggest changes on any document, from fixing a typo to a doing statute rewrite.
      • Residents who link their Saskatchewan Account to their Health Card will be allowed to make suggested changes and "like" or "support" other people's suggested changes.
        • See more about accounts and digital access in Policy 16 - Digital Sovereignty.
      • Suggested changes will be viewed as official petitions to the government.
        • Suggestions with 5000 Saskatchewan Resident "likes" will receive a Ministerial Response within 60 days of reaching 5000 "likes." Suggestions with 15,000 Saskatchewan Residents "likes" will be brought forward to the Legislative Assembly by the Speaker for a debate and vote on the matter.
      • Suggested changes can only be implemented by the relevant high level authority for the area the change was made.
      • Suggested changes, such as an amendment to a law, can only be approved and changed by Legislative Assembly majority vote.
      • Suggested change author's will be cited as an official author alongside lawmakers should their proposal be accepted.
    • Uploading to the repo:
      • All non-private government documents, data, and draft documents will be uploaded to the repo in no more than 48 hours.
      • Personal data and correspondence will be excluded for being required to upload.
      • For every document or other data uploaded to the repo, the following are required to ensure accurate records are kept.
        • Immutable Tags:
          • Author Name.
          • Author Role (Minister, MLA, Deputy Minister, Public Servant, etc.).
          • Author Party Affiliation (if applicable).
          • Date Created.
          • Date Published.
          • Change Log (what changed, when, and why).
        • Plain language Summary of what changed.
        • Machine readable schemas.
        • Digital Signatures.
        • Public Checksums.
