The Problem

People don’t own their work and lose out on the value they create.

The Promise

The NCP will make sure it’s easy to own your own work and your own value in Saskatchewan.

The Plan

Within 12 months of forming government, the NCP will introduce two Acts:

  • The Saskatchewan Right of Worker First Refusal Act.
  • The Saskatchewan Co-operative Development Agency Act

These acts will give people the tools they need to own their work, own their value, and own their dignity.

The Right of First Refusal Act:

Including amendments to existing laws, this act will ensure:

  • Employees who have worked for more than one year at any business in Saskatchewan, collectively with other employees of the same or higher seniority, have the Right of First Refusal to purchase the business that employs them during any type of sale or closure.
  • All Business Owners must file a Notice of Intent with the Fair Work Authority (FWA) whenever they intend to:
    • Sell a controlling share of the business, or
    • Sell substantially all of its assets, or
    • Pause operations for a long-term timeframe, or
    • Permanently close the business, or
    • File for bankruptcy and receivership.
  • None of the above can be legally completed until the FWA gives the final approval.
    • See more about the Fair Work Authority (FWA) in policy 09 - Fair Labour
  • The information included in the Notice of Intent must include:
    • What exactly the Business Owners intend and the reason why.
    • Current employee names.
    • Current employee addresses.
    • Current employee contact information.
    • The last five years of financial information.
    • Current leases.
    • Current contractors.
    • Other key information needed for business operation.
  • The FWA, under strict privacy protections and only for the purposes of notifying employees of their rights, must then inform in the Saskatchewan Co-operative Development Agency of what the Business Owner intends, and hand over the employee contact information.
    • The SCDA will contact and inform each employee about what is happening, and their right to buy the business and form it into a co-op collectively.
    • The SCDA will provide supports to employees who want to make a serious bid.
  • Following employee notification by the SCDA, Business Owners must grant a 120 day exclusivity window to their employees to make a bid.
    • Business Owners cannot take any other offers during this time.
    • Should no attempted bid be made by the employees the exclusivity offer ends and the Business Owners are free to sell to anyone.
    • However, should a bid be made by the employees, the Business Owners must consider it in good faith.
    • Further, any bona fide third-party offer in the next two years following the 120 day exclusivity window, must be disclosed so that employees may match it.
      • If the offers are substantially the same between any third party and the employees, the Business Owners must choose the employee offer.
  • Related-party and below-market deals are considered as full sales subject to the Right of First Refusal and FWA scrutiny.

The Saskatchewan Co-operative Development Agency Act:

Including amendments to existing laws, this Act will ensure:

  • The Saskatchewan Co-operative Development Agency (SCDA) will be mandated to help anyone form a co-op in Saskatchewan, be it newly made or converting an existing business, by providing a one stop shop of regular support, financial brokering, and conversion support.
  • The SCDA will not support co-ops whose primary activity is fossil fuel extraction and expansion.
  • Employees who have worked for more than one year at any business in Saskatchewan, collectively with other employees of the same or higher seniority, have the Right of First Refusal to purchase the business that employs them during any type of sale or closure.
  • Templates will be provided for regular requirements of every co-op or business in a variety of areas, including:
    • Regulations
    • Bylaws
    • Membership agreements
    • Payroll
    • Board Training
    • Board charters
    • Voting rules
    • Market access
    • Marketing
    • Annual Meetings
    • Conflict of Interest Policies
  • The SCDA will act as a brokerage, working with local banks, credit unions, or the new Saskatchewan Financial Group to find the best possible rates for co-ops requiring business loans for operations or for conversions.
    • The SCDA will not take deposits or issues any loans itself.
  • The SCDA will have dedicated units for helping to convert existing businesses into co-ops, in particular those exercising their Right of First Refusal, by helping current employees purchase their place of employment.
  • The SCDA will provide workshops and training to train new or potential co-op owners so they can hit the ground running.
  • Fees for services by the SCDA will be kept low for small or start up co-ops and scale up based on size of co-op and complexity of any buyouts.

The Funding

The funding will come out of General Revenues as well as consulting and brokerage fees from the Saskatchewan Co-operative Development Agency.
