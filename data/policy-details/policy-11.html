The Problem

Somehow we have an unemployment crisis and also a worker shortage.

The Promise

The NCP will hire, build, and train the workforce of tomorrow, by doing the work of today.

The Plan

Within 12 months of forming government, the NCP will introduce The Saskatchewan Century Corps Act.

The Act will introduce a new Crown corporation to be known as the Saskatchewan Century Corps (SCC) that will be mandated to be open to all, no matter the ability, to achieve the following objectives:

  • Provide stable public employment in priority sectors.
  • Deliver paid, on the job, training that leads to recognized credentials.
  • Deliver paid, in the classroom or lab, training that leads to recognized credentials.
  • Provide streamlined on-ramps, including accessibility employment tracks, with extra supports and modified expectations for those who need extra help to serve their province.
  • Support the Just Transition of workers into low carbon and care economies.
  • Maintain a deployable reserve for provincially declared emergencies.
  • Respect FPIC and co-develop projects affecting Indigenous Lands and Peoples.

How it Works:

  • The SCC is the employer of record, centralizing things like EI, CPP, WCB, and benefits.
  • Credentials are granted externally, outside the SCC.
    • The SCC arranges and pays for training and hours.
    • Some SCC supervisors will be approved as assessors / validators by the relevant credentialing bodies so they may sign off on hours and competencies.
  • Work is provided through Standing Master Agreements.
    • The Government of Saskatchewan, through its Crowns, Ministries, and Agencies, will provide agreements and contracts to the SCC for work in needed, relevant, areas.
  • Public Rate Cards will be posted, listing:
    • Labour Categories and Crew types.
    • Overhead.
    • Travel / per diems.
    • Gear.
  • The SCC will not underbid qualified bidders.
    • Where private or co-op bidders meet or exceed SCC wage and benefit floors, the SCC will not undercut them on price. Where bidders pay below SCC standards, the SCC may compete to ensure work is performed at a fair-wage and benefit standard.
  • Where feasible, secondments and subcontracts be used.
    • Priority will be given to secondments and subcontracts with public entities, non-profits, and co-ops, with private firms engaged where it supports training, apprenticeships, and local capacity.
  • The compensation:
    • Employees begins as paid employment from day one, including the bootcamp and all training.
    • No SCC employee will be paid less than $30 / hour, with higher scales in skilled occupations.
    • Target pay will be at or above the median wage for comparable work in the sector the SCC employee works in.
    • Premiums will be granted for:
      • Rural / Northern placements.
      • Speaking a second language.
      • Speaking an Indigenous Language.
      • Hard to staff positions and roles.
  • The Schedules:
    • The SCC will follow the new full time, 30 hour week, schedule found in policy 09 - Fair Labour.
  • An option to become an Emergency Reservist will be offered to SCC employees.
    • Opt-in to be placed on a paid reservist list.
    • Deployable on declared provincial emergencies.
    • Two paid re-training days a year will be mandatory.
  • Free, Prior, and Informed Consent:
    • FPIC must be achieved for any and all projects affecting Indigenous lands, waters, communities, or cultures.
    • See more in policy 04 - Reconciliation.
  • Safety Requirements:
    • SCC must maintain COR/ISO equivalent safety programs.
    • OHS representatives must be on the Board.
  • Equity Targets:
    • The SCC will have portfolio targets specifically for underrepresented groups, such as:
      • Women.
      • Indigenous individuals.
      • Northern / Rural individuals.
      • New Comers.
      • People with disabilities.
      • Youth under 26 years of age.

The Loop:

  • Bootcamp:
    • Paid training over a 6 week period that new employees must go through.
    • The first three weeks are dedicated to basic employment training, including things such as:
      • First Aid and CPR.
      • Mental Health First Aid.
      • WHMIS / TDG.
      • ICS-100.
      • Disaster Response.
      • Respirator fit testing.
      • Mandatory full immunizations.
      • and more.
    • The second three weeks are dedicated to training for the specific job placement.
  • Work, Train, Learn Repeat:
    • 3 days of the week are on the job site, actively working and learning.
    • 2 days of the week are in the lab for further specific instruction and learning, as well as taking or studying for credentialed exams.
    • This schedule may change during emergencies.
  • Yearly Review:
    • An annual review will take place for each employee of the SCC to review:
      • If the employee is happy and satisfied in their role and trajectory.
      • How well the employee is doing in the job and if they need more training time.
    • Return or Leave:
      • Employees will then be given the option to either:
        • Continue with the SCC, climb the ladder.
        • Transition to University or trade school using their earned Tuition Voucher(s).
        • Begin a new loop with a different role in the SCC.
        • Successfully leave the SCC for the private sector with new skills.

Tuition Vouchers:

  • Every year of honourable service in the SCC will earn an employee one Tuition Voucher.
    • A Tuition Voucher covers the cost of one full academic year at any public University or Trade school partner in Saskatchewan, including the costs of:
      • Two full-time academic terms.
      • Books.
      • Fees.
      • Bus pass.
    • Tuition Vouchers will be designed as non-taxable education grants under federal tax law.
    • Tuition Vouchers have no expiration date.
    • Tuition Vouchers cannot be transferred.
    • Tuition Vouchers can be used to pay off existing student loan debt, federal or provincial by payment to the NSLSC.
    • Tuition value capped by agreements with public institutions.

Initial Corps Streams:

  • The initial streams of the SCC will be:
    • The Saskatchewan Operations and Logistics (O&L) Corps.
    • The Saskatchewan Environmental and Resilience (E&R) Corps.
    • The Saskatchewan Care and Community (C&C) Corps.
    • The Saskatchewan Infrastructure and Housing (I&H) Corps.
    • The Saskatchewan Digital and Tech (D&T) Corps.
    • The Saskatchewan Agriculture and Aquaculture (A&A) Corps.
  • Work will be categorized and distributed through these streams.

Governance and Union:

  • The first order of business for the SCC is to determine a union. It may either fold itself into the SGEU or create its own union, with locals for each Corps Stream.
  • A CEO will be appointed to serve at the pleasure of the Board with Minister concurrence.
  • The Board must include representation from:
    • Indigenous governing bodies and organizations.
    • Labour Unions.
    • Municipalities.
    • Crowns/Ministries.
    • Education Partners (The Universities and Sask Polytech).
    • Safety and OHS.
  • Dashboards must be created and updated weekly to provide stats on such SCC work progress.

The Funding

The vast majority of funding will come from the General Revenue Fund, with contract revenue returning to the GRF.

Overtime, SCC contract revenue and productivity gains in other Crowns and Ministries will partially offset its operating costs.
