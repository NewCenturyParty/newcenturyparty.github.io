Saskatchewan 21st Century Initiative

About the Initiative

The Initiative is a community-built roadmap to guarantee human dignity, rebuild public institutions, and grow resilient Prairie communities. It brings the ideas from the 21st Century Act to life with Saskatchewan-focused delivery plans.

Guiding Commitments

Dignity First

Every policy begins with the simple promise that people deserve care, shelter, and voice. The Initiative makes the Right to Human Dignity enforceable law and designs services to deliver it.

Co-Governance

Free, Prior, and Informed Consent is baked into energy, housing, and infrastructure plans. Indigenous governments are decision-makers, not stakeholders.

Transparency

Dashboards, open data, and CodeShare Civics ensure every resident can see costs, metrics, and commitments. Public services are accountable in real time.

How We Work

Listening Sprints

Residents, frontline workers, Elders, and organizers share what is broken and what already works. Notes from these sessions shape each bill.

Design Studios

Teams prototype policy architecture—funding models, mandates, and accountability loops—so ideas can be handed to legislators and community partners.

Publish & Iterate

Drafts go public for review. The Initiative welcomes issue spotting, red-teaming, and better ideas through GitHub and regular briefings.

What Success Looks Like

  • Zero-dollar healthcare and pharmacare at point of care, tracked on public dashboards.
  • Safe, efficient homes built fast with SaskHomes plans and SaskEnergy thermal loops.
  • Transit that connects every town, bundling freight, groceries, and passengers together.
  • Saskatchewan residents steering their own data, Crown corporations, and civic processes.
