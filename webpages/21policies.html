The Platform

21 Policies

For A

21st Century
Saskatchewan.

01

Human Dignity

  • Enact the Saskatchewan Human Dignity Act affirming dignity as inviolable law.
  • Apply the duty to respect dignity across ministries, crowns, municipalities, and contractors.
  • Add gender identity, neurodiversity, disability, and social condition to Code protections.
  • Allow people harmed to seek Court of King's Bench remedies once appeals are exhausted.
02

The Saskatchewan Dignity Dividend

  • Monthly benefit up to $1,667 for residents earning under $100,000.
  • Benefit tapers $0.20 per dollar earned with a $50 monthly floor.
  • Opt-in annual application with mid-year reassessment for income loss.
  • Paid tax-free by direct deposit; replaces SIS and avoids clawbacks.
03

Universal Health

  • Bring your health card - single payer covers medically necessary care province-wide.
  • Include pharmacare, dental, vision, mental health, hearing, childcare, home and long-term care.
  • Clinician-led Benefit Councils set coverage rules, fee guides, and appeals.
  • Recruit and train providers with scholarships, SCC support, and expanded scopes.
  • Funded through a Universal Health Levy on payroll for employers and workers.
04

Reconciliation

  • Co-develop every action with Indigenous governments, honoring Treaties and UNDRIP.
  • Implement all 94 Calls to Action with a strong Reconciliation Commissioner.
  • Launch the Saskatchewan Council for Reconciliation to run consent protocols and audits.
  • Require Free, Prior, and Informed Consent with capacity, jobs, and revenue sharing.
  • Finance the work through the Saskatchewan Sovereignty Fund's Reconciliation Fund.
05

Housing Crisis

  • Freeze residential rents for five years and enforce a tenant bill of rights.
  • Create a provincial rental registry, winter eviction ban, and emergency rent grants.
  • Designate Priority Housing Zones with by-right multi-unit zoning and fast permits.
  • Rebuild public stock through SHC pre-leases, rapid rehab, and housing-first placements.
  • Stand up SaskHomes modular factories and retrofit kits funded by bonds and taxes.
06

21st Century Connected Communities

  • Convert SaskEnergy into a thermal utility building fifth-generation ambient loop networks.
  • End new gas hookups by 2030 while coordinating dig-once upgrades with SaskPower and SaskTel.
  • Deploy SaskPower microgrids, EV chargers, rooftop solar, and household batteries with on-bill options.
  • Tie municipal grants to 20-minute communities, protected bike lanes, and land value tax reforms.
  • Rebuild STC as a provincewide freight-and-transit backbone toward high-speed rail.
07

Public Safety

  • Disband Saskatchewan Marshals and send crisis teams first to non-violent 911 calls.
  • Rewrite the Police Act to require de-escalation, ban carding, and enforce body cameras.
  • Guarantee housing-first placements and municipal services for encampments until homes are ready.
  • Decriminalize simple possession with 24/7 sobering centres, OAT access, and naloxone saturation.
  • Redirect policing budgets, SaskBonds, and partners to fund harm reduction and community safety.
08

Co-operative Development

  • Give workers first right to buy when businesses sell or close, with a 120-day window.
  • Require sale notices, fair appraisals, and registry proof before any transfer is registered.
  • Create the Saskatchewan Co-operative Development Agency as a single access point.
  • Provide financing guarantees, legal teams, and training to convert firms into co-ops.
  • Target 30 percent of Saskatchewan enterprises becoming co-ops within ten years.
09

Fair Labour

  • Adopt a Worker Bill of Rights with a $25 minimum wage and 30-hour full-time standard.
  • Protect pay, benefits, overtime tiers, rest periods, vacations, and 15 paid sick days.
  • Limit probation, mandate severance, and enforce equal pay across job classifications.
  • Stand up an independent Fair Work Authority with inspections, AMPs, and payroll credits.
  • Expand apprenticeships and SaskJobs support, plus transition grants for small employers.
10

Just Transition

  • Cover oil and gas workers, contractors, and dependents with 12 months recent service.
  • Offer retire, retrain, or redeploy pathways tailored to each worker's choice.
  • Guarantee income bridges, tuition coverage, childcare, relocation, and tool support.
  • Provide wage insurance and SCC placements to land comparable jobs within 30 days.
  • Have the Just Transition Office audit files, hear appeals, and stop double dipping.
11

The Saskatchewan Century Corps

  • Establish the Saskatchewan Century Corps as a Crown employer of record.
  • Deliver paid training, credentials, and $30 per hour jobs in priority sectors.
  • Use master agreements to place crews across ministries, crowns, co-ops, and municipalities.
  • Offer tuition vouchers, automatic raises, and flexible 30-hour scheduling.
  • Maintain an emergency reserve and secure FPIC for any project on Indigenous lands.
12

Lifelong Learning

  • Raise per-student funding to $21,000, cap class sizes, and boost educator pay.
  • Guarantee prep time, classroom supplies, and specialist staffing in every school.
  • Co-create curriculum on civics, digital literacy, consent, climate, and Indigenous languages.
  • Build the Saskatchewan Library Authority with one card, maker labs, and device lending.
  • Expand SDLC for adult education, tutoring, accessibility, and homeschool supports.
13

Climate Crisis

  • End fossil fuel subsidies and stop approving new wells, pipelines, or refineries.
  • Hold producers liable for cleanup with fines, asset seizures, and SCC backstops.
  • Deploy farm energy retrofits, regenerative practices, and fertilizer efficiency programs.
  • Scale SaskEnergy ambient loops, deep weatherization, and right-to-connect heat pumps.
  • Finance the shift through SaskBonds, progressive taxes, and net-neutral on-bill options.
14

Food Sovereignty

  • Create the Saskatchewan Agriculture Board with crop price floors and deficiency payments.
  • Run voluntary pools, regenerative dividends, and fast grading dispute resolution.
  • Require FPIC, ban supplier retaliation, and protect farmer data privacy.
  • Launch SaskGrocery formats (super, market, mini, mobile) to end food deserts.
  • Cap margins on basics, eliminate food waste, and enforce an agricultural right to repair.
15

Resource and Energy Sovereignty

  • Form the Saskatchewan Natural Resource Corporation with Indigenous co-governance.
  • Compete or partner on critical minerals, geothermal, helium, and midstream projects.
  • Keep permitting level with private peers while sharing revenues and jobs through FPIC.
  • Task SaskPower with layered grids, virtual power plants, nuclear, solar, wind, and storage.
  • Offer appliance replacement grants and on-bill financing to electrify homes and businesses.
16

Digital Sovereignty

  • Require Saskatchewan residency for storing or processing sensitive government data.
  • Adopt open-source-first tech and ban vendors from training on provincial datasets.
  • Roll out SaskOS Linux across ministries within ten years with accessible design.
  • Empower Saskatchewan Digital Services to build sovereign apps, portals, and civic repositories.
  • Launch secure SaskTel email for government and the public with passkeys and MFA.
17

Financial Sovereignty

  • Build the Saskatchewan Sovereignty Fund as a permanent, ring-fenced endowment.
  • Dedicate royalty surpluses to Dignity, Resilience, and Reconciliation funds with 36-month buffers.
  • Activate windfall levies and crown dividend redirects when the SDD reserve dips.
  • Create the Saskatchewan Financial Group with personal, co-op, and development divisions.
  • Guarantee no-fee banking, worker buyout loans, green financing, and strict risk oversight.
18

Democratic Renewal

  • Adopt mixed-member proportional representation with voting at 16 and mandatory ballots.
  • Use paper ballots, election weeks, and Sainte-Lague seat allocations with overhang controls.
  • Force lobbying into public forums with real-time disclosures and ban paid consultants.
  • Ban corporate and union donations, cap individual gifts, and speed up reporting.
  • Publish laws and consultations on an open civic repository with verified petition tools.
19

Manufacturing

  • Establish the Saskatchewan Manufacturing Corporation to build green industrial capacity.
  • Deliver modular SaskHomes production within three years using licensed designs.
  • Assemble heat pumps, renewable hardware, and batteries with closed-loop standards.
  • Partner with Indigenous nations under FPIC and publish transparent performance metrics.
  • Finance growth through SaskBonds, general revenues, and reinvested profits.
20

Disaster Resilience

  • Equip resilience hubs with clean air, supplies, and 72-hour islandable power.
  • Locate hubs in schools, libraries, rec centres, and halls within 30 minutes of residents.
  • Create SERA to coordinate incident command, depots, and mandatory emergency plans.
  • Train Saskatchewan Century Corps reserves for wildfire, flood, and disaster deployments.
  • Mandate islandable power for critical sites by 2030 using bonds and shared funding.
21

Funding: Taxes, Royalties, and METR

  • Increase personal tax brackets by 0.5 to 1.5 points and add priority profession credits.
  • Introduce progressive corporate brackets with reinvestment incentives and anti-avoidance rules.
  • Raise base and windfall royalties, sending surpluses to the Saskatchewan Sovereignty Fund divisions.
  • Expand SaskBonds with labeled climate, social, and savings series starting at $100.
  • Send crown dividends above five-year averages to the SSF and publish annual METR curves.
