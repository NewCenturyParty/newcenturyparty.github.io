HOW WE PAY

Four Pillars

We fund the 21 Policies with a mix of fair taxation, shared resource wealth, and public investment tools. Each pillar is designed to grow with Saskatchewan’s economy and make sure prosperity is felt across every community.

Progressive Personal Taxes

  • Increase existing brackets by 0.5–1.5 points so the highest earners contribute more.

  • Add new top brackets while creating Priority Professional Tax Credits for nurses, doctors, educators, and other essential workers.

  • Index brackets and credits to annual CPI so families are protected from inflation.

Fair Corporate Contributions

  • Introduce progressive provincial corporate brackets focused on active business income.

  • Reward firms that reinvest in wages, in-province R&D, and accredited training through a Reinvestment Tax Credit.

  • Apply anti-fragmentation rules and a provincial GAAR so related entities cannot dodge their obligations.

Resource Royalties & Sovereignty Fund

  • Simplify royalties with higher base and windfall rates, targeting at least 35% of profits for residents.

  • Split revenues between the General Revenue Fund and the Saskatchewan Sovereignty Fund for Dignity, Resilience, and Reconciliation.

  • Layer a three-year Sovereignty Surcharge to seed the Dignity Dividend and future-proof provincial finances.

SaskBonds & Crown Dividends

  • Issue labelled SaskBonds: Climate, Social, and Investment, with second-party opinions and ring-fenced proceeds.

  • Offer $100 minimums, payroll deductions, and an online portal so every resident can invest locally.

  • Require crown corporations to remit their five-year average dividend to the General Revenue Fund and direct surpluses into the Sovereignty Fund.

Guardrails

Accountability is built into every dollar. These safeguards keep the plan transparent, disciplined, and focused on building prosperity that lasts.

Transparent Budgets

Publish five-year operating and capital plans for each program before launch, with quarterly open-data dashboards so residents can follow the money.

Pay-As-We-Build

No major project breaks ground without identified revenue. Royalties and labelled SaskBonds stay ring-fenced until allocated and audited.

METR Discipline

The Finance Minister tables combined METR curves every year and must deliver corrective measures within 60 days if the rate exceeds 55% through the Dignity Dividend phase-out.

Sovereignty Fund Stewardship

Resource revenues above the five-year average and crown dividend surpluses flow to the Saskatchewan Sovereignty Fund, building the Dignity, Resilience, and Reconciliation accounts.
