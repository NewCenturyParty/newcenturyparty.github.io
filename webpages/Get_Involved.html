Policy 18 - Democratic Renewal

What Is CodeShare Civics?

Most political parties write their platforms behind closed doors and present them to voters as finished products. The NCP does the opposite. Every policy lives in a public Git repository, where anyone can read the full draft, track every change, propose edits, and flag problems.

When enough residents support a proposed change, the relevant ministry must respond. Every commit is traceable. Every decision is auditable. The history of how a policy developed is public record, forever.

Think of it as Git for governance. Pull requests as petitions. Open branches as draft legislation. The canonical mirror as the official gazette.

This is not a gimmick. It is a genuine attempt to prototype the democratic infrastructure Saskatchewan deserves, built in the open, before we are even in government, so you can see exactly how it works.

Four Steps. No Experience Required.

You do not need to know how to code. You need to know Saskatchewan.

01 - Explore

Read the existing policy notes in the repository. Review the public summary on the website. Understand what has already been proposed and why.

02 - Draft

Create or edit Markdown files in the relevant policy folder. Plain language first. Cite your sources. One policy per contribution, keep it focused.

03 - Discuss

Open or join a GitHub Issue to ask questions, flag trade-offs, or challenge assumptions. This is where real deliberation happens, publicly, on the record.

04 - Review

Submit a pull request. Receive feedback. Iterate until there is consensus. At least one reviewer must approve before anything merges. Everything is version-controlled.

Whether you are writing your first sentence or refining an entire policy, you are welcome here. These policies belong to everyone who shows up to build them.
