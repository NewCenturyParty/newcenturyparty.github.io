Who we are

Hope For The Future

The New Century Party started with neighbours who saw our province facing crises we could not solve alone. We are building a public, people-powered blueprint for shared prosperity—rooted in prairie cooperation, accountable to everyone who calls this place home.

We are small, on purpose: this is the moment to sketch the ideas, test them in the open, and invite builders, workers, caregivers, and creators to make the plan better.

The

New Century Party

We stand in the tradition of Saskatchewan people who built what they needed together. From community hospitals to crowns that kept the lights on, we have always been bold enough to design our own future.

Today's challenges—climate shocks, economic inequality, democratic fatigue—demand the same courage. The NCP is drafting 21 Policies for the 21st Century Saskatchewan, inviting the province to stress-test them, improve them, and hold us to account.

There is no leader waiting in the wings. This party becomes real only when residents, workers, and communities decide to build it.

What Guides Us

We design policy like co-operatives build barns—transparent, inclusive, and grounded in the realities of prairie life.

Shared Prosperity

Every dollar of wealth Saskatchewan generates should circulate back to the people who create it—through universal services, thriving local businesses, and strong rural and urban communities.

Transparency First

Budgets, drafts, and data are published in the open. When we make a claim, residents can see the math, question the assumptions, and shape the next iteration.

Prairie Pragmatism

We honour Saskatchewan’s tradition of rolling up sleeves and building what works—combining proven public tools with new institutions ready for a changing world.

Partnership Over Partisanship

Farmers, educators, technologists, care workers, and students all have a stake. We craft policy with them, not for them, so solutions last longer than election cycles.
