The Xanatos Gambit

A Xanatos Gambit is a plan where every possible outcome benefits the planner. If opponents resist, they lose. If they comply, you win. If they do nothing, you still advance.

The Saskatchewan 21 Policies are not twenty-one separate ideas. They are one interlocking system designed so that every response by every actor — pay or don’t pay, cooperate or resist, stay or leave — advances the transformation of Saskatchewan. Every fork in the road leads to the same destination.

Policy 01

THE FOUNDATION:
HUMAN DIGNITY

It starts here. The Saskatchewan Human Dignity Act establishes that the primary fiduciary duty of the Government of Saskatchewan is to respect, protect, and uphold Human Dignity. All other enactments must be read consistently with this Act.

This is not symbolic. It is the legal backbone of the entire platform. Every policy that follows — housing, food, energy, labour, reconciliation — flows from this duty. If the Government has a duty to uphold Human Dignity, then it has a duty to act on the rest of the S21P.

You cannot promise dignity and then refuse to deliver it.

The Opening Move

The Redwater Strategy

Immediately upon forming government, the Minister of Environment updates the Liability Management Ratio (LMR) to 2.0 for all oil and gas companies operating in Saskatchewan. Their assets must be worth at least double their environmental liabilities — the abandoned wells, the aging pipelines, the tailings ponds, all the mess they have left behind.

The difference between their current actual LMR and the new 2.0 standard must be posted as a cash bond. No credit. No corporate promises. Cold, hard, cash. Twenty-five percent within 90 days. The remainder within 12 months.

This creates the first fork.

Fork A: They Pay

If the oil companies post their bonds, that is potentially tens to hundreds of billions of dollars placed in trust for environmental remediation. That trust is held in the Saskatchewan Financial Group, where it accrues interest for the province before being used for cleanup. The interest income flows to the Saskatchewan Sovereignty Fund.


Saskatchewan gets funded remediation and a massive injection of capital into its own financial system. The companies continue operating under new, stricter rules.


Win.

Fork B: They Refuse

If a company cannot or refuses to post the bond, the regulator takes that as an admission that they cannot meet their statutory environmental obligations. They are, in the eyes of the law, insolvent with respect to their cleanup liabilities.


Their operating licenses are suspended. The assets are placed under provincial receivership through the Saskatchewan Natural Resources Corporation (SNRC) as operator of last resort.


Win.

The Monday Morning Problem

A New Century Party Government has no interest in running the oil and gas industry. As such, should the oil majors choose Fork B, the Province will turn around and offer them brand new operating licenses, with the following conditions:

  • Begin cleaning up orphaned wells immediately
  • Pay the Energy Veterans Surcharge (5.5% on all Saskatchewan revenues)
  • Clean up as they go — the land should be so clean their children could farm it
  • A 15-year sunset on all oil and gas operations, with extraction permits declining 10% annually for years 1–10 and 20% annually for years 11–15

The NCP is confident the oil majors will take the deal. But if they truly will not act in the best interests of their customers, the Government of Saskatchewan will activate contingency operations within 24 hours to guarantee continuity.

We would never let our friends go cold.

If they refuse these terms, they are the ones who have to explain to the White House and the American Mid-West why gas prices are about to triple and why homes will freeze.

No matter what, the province wins.

Policy 10

THE WORKFORCE

The 15-year sunset does not abandon workers. It honours them.

Energy Veterans built and powered the modern world. They kept homes warm in −40 and fuelled the industries that built modern Saskatchewan. We honour that service with a platinum parachute and three voluntary pathways out: Retire, Retrain, or Redeploy.

No matter which pathway a worker chooses, every Energy Veteran receives:

  • $15,000 Energy Veterans Bonus
  • Saskatchewan Prosperity Medal honouring service and contribution
  • Debt Shield (4 years) — the Government of Saskatchewan reimburses 100% of the interest on a worker’s primary residential mortgage and one primary vehicle

Three Pathways Out

Retire

For workers ready to finish with dignity: pension bridge support for up to six years, until OAS/CPP or full unreduced pension eligibility.

Retrain

Full scholarship, wage replacement, and childcare support for workers training into priority sectors like geothermal, nuclear, and advanced trades.

Redeploy

Direct placement into Saskatchewan employers that need these exact skills now, with pension/ticket parity and wage top-ups during the transition.

We aren’t asking drillers to code. We are asking them to keep drilling — but for geothermal boreholes instead of oil wells. Keep welding — but for CANDU reactor piping instead of pipelines. Same skills. More security. And they get to sleep in their own beds at the end of the day.

Policy 04

The Indigenous Shield

This is not a white boy telling Treaty Nations what to do. This is an invitation to co-govern. Not consult. Not advise. Co-govern. Two Sovereigns working together as equals.

The Consent Protocol establishes that before any project, policy, or action that affects Indigenous lands, communities, waters, heritage, culture, language, or livelihoods, the relevant Indigenous governing body must give Free, Prior, and Informed Consent (FPIC), as per UNDRIP. This must include cost sharing, revenue sharing, job opportunities, and capacity funding.

The BenefitThis partnership eliminates two of the biggest risks in resource development: protest risk and lawsuit risk. When Indigenous Nations are co-owners and co-governors with veto power, there is no one left to protest and no one left to sue. That means more certainty, faster timelines, and lower costs for everyone. Security and confidence, built into the foundation.

The TrapIf the Government of Canada, or any international body, tries to stop the S21P, they are not just going up against a provincial government. They are going up against Indigenous Title. They are going up against FPIC. They are going up against UNDRIP, which Canada itself has adopted. The Canadian government would have to argue against Indigenous self-determination to stop Saskatchewan’s platform. That is politically, legally, and internationally untenable. Checkmate.

Policy 15

Uranium OPEC

Saskatchewan has the highest-grade uranium deposits in the world. Under the current model, we ship raw yellowcake — uranium dirt that is literally yellow — out of the province and buy back the processed fuel. No more.

Saskatchewan will become the uranium OPEC. We mine it, process it, fabricate it, burn it, and sell the excess at a dignity-based price. The entire value chain will belong to us, and we will dictate the terms.

To do so, the SNRC and the Saskatchewan Manufacturing Corporation (SMC) will build a uranium conversion facility and a fuel fabrication plant in northern Saskatchewan. Policy 15 introduces a Value-Add Levy that makes the raw export of yellowcake economically uncompetitive with domestic processing.

Energy IndependenceOur self-made fuel bundles will power Saskatchewan’s new fleet of CANDU nuclear reactors, with excess fuel and electrons sold to other Provinces and States. Private companies will have the chance to partner with Saskatchewan in building these facilities to ensure their own supply needs are met, otherwise they can buy directly from us, at a dignity-based price.

Non-ProliferationSaskatchewan’s CANDU pathway is built on unenriched uranium. That means zero proliferation risk. That means we can do this.

Medical IsotopesCANDU reactors produce Molybdenum-99, used in cancer treatment and medical imaging worldwide.

We will flood the market with these isotopes to bring the price down and help cure disease cheaply.

Policy 14

Food
Sovereignty

Saskatchewan is the breadbasket of Canada. Grocery chains are making records profits. Yet our farmers are struggling to feed their families. That is unacceptable and it stops now.

The New Century Party will put the value chain in the hands of farmers, where it belongs.

The SAB

The Saskatchewan Agriculture Board (SAB) will offer voluntary commodity pools with a Spring Time Advance of up to 75% of projected crop value upfront, interest-free. No more exorbitant loans just to do the work.

An independent Grading Arbitration Office will be established to ensure farmers never get ripped off again.

And the SAB will operate a Farmland Trust with the Right of First Refusal on all farmland in Saskatchewan, offered to Saskatchewan Farmer's at fair market value. No more Bay Street farms.

Saskatchewan holds nearly a third of the world’s potash supply. The SAB will create a strategic reserve for potash, nitrogen, and finished fertilizer, sold cheap to our farmers at wholesale.

SaskGrocery

The Saskatchewan Grocery Corporation (SaskGrocery) will be established with a simple goal: bring down food prices, and eliminate the food deserts.

It will act as both a wholesaler and retailer, ensuring that fresh, local food is accessible to all Saskatchewan residents, even at their local independent grocer.

An Essentials Basket will be created containing basic necessities at affordable prices. Think bread, milk, eggs, flour, baby formula, seasonal produce, and more, all sold at landed cost + logistics + 1% and not a penny more.

And no matter where you live, be it Stony Rapids or downtown Regina, the Essentials Basket will cost the same.

No more geography taxes on the food you need to survive.

Policy 17

THE
FINANCIAL
FORTRESS

What the Big Banks and Ottawa think of all this will mean little to the people of Saskatchewan. We will have our own banking system backstopped by our own money.

The Fund

Saskatchewan Sovereignty Fund (SSF)

A permanent, intergenerational endowment fund, capitalized by 15% of all resource royalties and revenues, and 50% of windfall profits. Additionally, any further resource royalties revenues, or Crown corporation dividends, above a five year rolling average, will be deposited into the Fund annually. The principal is protected by a supermajority vote lock, so it cannot be spent by short-sighted politicians. Annual interest is deposited into the General Revenue Fund in ring-fenced quantities, ensuring the money is spent intentionally.

By Law

SSF Annual Interest Allocation

  • 54% Dignity Dividend
  • 15% Reconciliation
  • 15% Resilience & Energy Veterans
  • 10% Debt Servicing
  • 5% Strategic Investment
  • 1% Administration
The Depository

Saskatchewan Financial Group (SFG)

The SFG is not a consumer bank and will not offer retail banking services. It is a public depository and liquidity platform by design, which avoids federal bank-style operating constraints while keeping Saskatchewan’s public cash under Saskatchewan control.

Over 36 months, the Province draws down dependency on the Big Five and brings the public-sector float home. Today, Saskatchewan cash funds Bay Street lending and Toronto condos. Under the SFG, that same float is used here to backstop and fund Saskatchewan's credit unions so they can compete harder, lend more, and keep value in-province.

42% of Sask Residents use a Credit Union.

If the banks or credit rating agencies get a little Moody and downgrade Saskatchewan's Credit Rating...

A NCP government would have no choice but to pull the entire float, estimated at $3 Billion, on a random Tuesday morning, causing the largest capital shock in Canadian history.

So, we suggest they play ball.

Legal Foundation

Research Notes

Tsilhqot’in Nation v. British Columbia (2014)

Established that Aboriginal title is a beneficial interest in the land itself. The Crown must obtain consent from the title-holding group before using the land. Strengthens the Indigenous Shield.

Yahey v. British Columbia (2021)

Found that the cumulative effects of industrial development infringed Treaty 8 rights. Establishes precedent for climate and environmental damages linked to Treaty rights violations.

Orphan Well Association v. Grant Thornton (Redwater, 2019)

Supreme Court of Canada ruled that provincial environmental cleanup obligations take priority over the claims of creditors in bankruptcy. Validates the Redwater Strategy’s legal foundation.

Natural Resources Transfer Act (1930)

Transferred control of Crown lands and natural resources to the prairie provinces. Saskatchewan’s constitutional authority over its resources is the bedrock of the entire platform.
