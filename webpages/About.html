The New Century Party

The Future Is Built Together

We are a Saskatchewan political party with one foundational belief: every person in this province deserves to live with dignity. Not someday. Now.

Who We Are

A Party Built on Hope

For too long, the people of Saskatchewan have been told a story about the future. A story where the best you can do is hang on. Where the goal is not to thrive, but to survive. Where the headlines are always about what is being cut, what is being lost, and what we cannot afford.

The New Century Party was founded because we believe Saskatchewan deserves a better story. One where the future is not something to be endured. It is something to be built. Together. With purpose. With ambition. With dignity at the centre of everything.

We are not here to manage decline. We are here to build a province that works for everyone who calls it home.

There Is an Alternative

For a generation, politics has repeated the same script: cut services, sell assets, trust the market, and accept that some people will be left behind. The NCP rejects that completely.

There is always an alternative when you have the courage to look for one and the will to build it.

Saskatchewan is resource-rich and people-rich. The problem has never been scarcity. The problem has been political imagination and the willingness to make our collective wealth serve the people who live here.

What We Stand For

Our Principles

Everything the New Century Party does flows from a small set of core commitments. These are the foundation every one of our 21 policies is built on.

01 - Human Dignity First

The primary duty of government is to respect, protect, and uphold the inherent dignity of every person. We will bind that duty in law through the Saskatchewan Human Dignity Act.

02 - Power Shared Is Power Secured

Real strength comes from distribution, not concentration. We support shared ownership, democratic institutions, and co-governance with Indigenous nations.

03 - Thriving, Not Just Surviving

The bar is not survival. The bar is a good life for everyone: universal care, secure housing, meaningful work, and a future people can imagine here.

04 - Built Here, For Here

Saskatchewan wealth should build Saskatchewan futures. Resources, food, energy, and data created here should circulate here to fund public prosperity.

05 - Reconciliation as Partnership

Reconciliation is shared authority, shared revenue, and shared decision-making. Not symbolism. Not lip service. Durable partnership between sovereign nations.

21 Policies. One Vision.

The Saskatchewan 21 Policies are not isolated promises. They are interlocking policies designed to reinforce each other so progress in one area accelerates progress across the province.

Foundation

Human Dignity Act, Dignity Dividend, Universal Health, and Reconciliation establish the legal and social floor for everyone.

Building

Public housing, connected communities, manufacturing capacity, and the Saskatchewan Century Corps create long-term public assets.

Working

Fair labour standards, just transition supports, co-operative development, and lifelong learning expand dignity through work.

Sovereignty

Food, resource, digital, and financial sovereignty keep value created in Saskatchewan circulating in Saskatchewan.

Resilience

Climate action, public safety reform, disaster preparedness, and democratic renewal make the system durable under stress.
